## ARTICLE I — DEFINITIONS

In construing these Bylaws:

- **1.1 "Board"** means the Board of Directors of this Association constituted in accordance with Article V of these Bylaws.
- **1.2 "Declaration"** means the Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions for SummitRidge Townhomes dated December 17, 1999, recorded in the official records of Multnomah County, Oregon on December 17, 1999 under Recording No. 99-225640, and all of the easements, covenants, restrictions and charges set forth therein, together with any rules or regulations promulgated thereunder, as the same may have been or may be amended or supplemented from time to time in accordance with the provisions thereof.
- **1.3 "Director"** means a director of the Association as described in and elected in accordance with Article V.
- **1.4 "Officer"** means an officer of the Association as described in and elected in accordance with Article VI.
- **1.5 "President"** means the President of the Association as described in Article VI, Section 5.
- **1.6 "Secretary"** means the Secretary of the Association as described in Article VI, Section 6.
- **1.7 "Turnover Date"** means the date on which Class B membership converts to Class A membership.
- **1.8 "Treasurer"** means the Treasurer of the Association as described in Article VI, Section 7.
- **1.9** Other undefined capitalized terms shall have the meanings given in the Declaration.

## ARTICLE II — OFFICES

The principal office of the Association in the State of Oregon shall be in a location determined by the Board. The Association may have such other offices, either within or without the State of Oregon, as the Board may determine or as the affairs of the Association require from time to time. The Association shall have and continuously maintain in the State of Oregon a registered office, and a registered agent whose office is identical with such registered office, as required by the nonprofit corporation laws of the State of Oregon. The registered office may be, but need not be, identical with the principal office in the State of Oregon, and the address of the registered office may be changed from time to time by the Board.

## ARTICLE III — MEMBERSHIP, VOTING RIGHTS, AND POWERS AND OBLIGATIONS

### Section 1. Membership

The Corporation will have Members. Members shall be every owner of a fee or an undivided fee interest in a Lot (as defined in the Declaration) subject by covenant of record to assessments by the Corporation, and every person who holds a contract purchaser's interest of record in a Lot. There shall be no other qualification for membership except as set forth above. Membership shall terminate on transfer of fee simple title in a Lot by a Member or transfer of the contract purchaser's interest in a Lot by the contract purchaser who qualifies as a Member. If a Member sells a Lot by contract of sale, the Member's membership shall terminate and the contract purchaser's membership shall commence upon recordation thereof.

### Section 2. Voting Rights and Classes of Voting Members

Voting rights shall be allocated among two classes of voting membership:

**A. Class A Members** — All Members other than SummitRidge Townhomes Associates, LLC (an Oregon limited liability company, the "Declarant"), the Class B Member. Class A Members are entitled to one vote for each Lot in which they hold the interest required for membership by Article III. If more than one person holds such interest or interests, all such persons shall be Members, but the vote for such Lot shall be exercised as the persons holding such interest shall determine between themselves; in no event shall more than one vote be cast with respect to any such Lot. If multiple owners of a single Lot cannot agree on the manner in which their vote shall be cast on any issue, then such owners shall be deemed to have abstained from voting on that issue. Class A Members shall not be entitled to elect any members of the Board of Directors so long as there is Class B membership.

**B. Class B Member** — The Declarant and any successor or assign of Declarant who acquires all of the interest of Declarant in the Property for purposes of completing development (if incomplete) and selling the balance of unsold Lots. The Class B Member is entitled to three votes for each Lot owned by the Class B Member. The Class B Member shall be entitled to elect all members of the Board of Directors until such time as Class B Membership is converted to Class A Membership. Class B Membership shall be converted to Class A upon the earliest of: (i) when Class A Members have a total number of votes in Association matters equal to or in excess of fifteen (15); (ii) December 31, 2002; and (iii) when Declarant, at its discretion, elects to convert by written notice to the Secretary.

### Section 3. Powers and Obligations

The Association shall have, exercise and perform the powers, duties and obligations granted by its Articles of Incorporation, the Declaration, and these Bylaws.

The powers and obligations of the Association may from time to time be amended, repealed, enlarged or restricted by changes in the Declaration made in accordance with the provisions therein, accompanied by changes in the Articles of Incorporation or these Bylaws made in accordance with such instruments and the nonprofit corporation laws of the State of Oregon.

## ARTICLE IV — MEETINGS OF MEMBERS

### Section 1. Annual Meetings Prior to Turnover Date
Prior to the Turnover Date, a meeting of Members shall be held annually, called in accordance with Section 3 below.

### Section 2. Meetings to Elect Directors; Annual Meeting Following Turnover Meeting
The first meeting of Members held to elect Directors pursuant to this Article IV shall be the Turnover Meeting (the initial meeting of the Association under the Oregon Planned Community Act, deemed the first annual meeting). Subsequent annual meetings shall be held on or about each anniversary of the initial annual meeting and called per Section 3.

### Section 3. Notice of Meeting
Meetings under this Article IV shall be held on such date, time and place within Multnomah or Clackamas County, Oregon, as designated by the Secretary. Written notice shall be given by, or at the direction of, the Secretary or other authorized person, by mailing a copy postage prepaid, **at least 15 days before but not more than 50 days before** the meeting, to each Member entitled to vote (addressed to the Member's address last appearing in Association records, or supplied by the Member). Mortgagees who request notice are also given notice. Mortgagees of a Lot may designate a representative to attend. Notice shall specify the place, day and hour of the meeting and the items on the agenda — including the general nature of any proposed amendment to the Declaration or Bylaws, any budget changes, or any proposal to remove a Director or Officer. Notice may be waived. No Member present at a meeting may object to the adequacy or timeliness of notice.

### Section 4. Proxies; Quorum; Voting
Each Member and Declarant shall have the votes provided in Article III, Section 2. Any Member may give a proxy to any person, so long as the proxy is in writing, signed by the Member, and filed with the Secretary. A proxy shall expire on the earlier of (a) 11 months after the date of the proxy or (b) the date of the sale of the Member's Lot. The presence (in person or by proxy) of Members entitled to cast at least **20 percent** of the total votes constitutes a quorum. Unless a greater percentage is required by law or the Declaration, the affirmative vote of a majority of votes represented and voting shall constitute the act of the Members. Voting may be by mail. For mail voting, the Secretary shall give written notice to all Members including (a) a written resolution setting forth the proposed action, (b) a statement that Members are entitled to vote by mail for or against, and (c) a date not less than **25 days** after notice by which votes must be received. Late votes are of no effect.

### Section 5. Annual Meetings
At any annual meeting, the President (and any other Officer the Board or President may designate) shall report on the activities and financial condition of the Association.

### Section 6. Special Meetings
Special meetings may be called at any time by the President or a majority of the Board, or upon written request of Members entitled to vote at least **25 percent** of the votes of Class A members.

## ARTICLE V — BOARD OF DIRECTORS

### Section 1. General
The affairs of the Association shall be managed by the Board, comprised of the number of Directors determined per Section 2. The Board has all requisite power, duty and authority under the Declaration, including the power to enforce its provisions and to acquire and pay for goods and services necessary or appropriate for proper functioning of the Association.

### Section 2. Number; Appointment by Declarant Prior to Turnover Meeting
Prior to the Turnover Meeting, Directors are not required to be Members; thereafter, all Directors shall be Members. The Board shall be comprised of **three Directors**. Until the Turnover Meeting, Declarant shall appoint all Directors. On the date of the Turnover Meeting, Declarant-appointed Directors shall submit their resignations effective upon the election of their successors. Voting for Directors shall not be cumulative.

### Section 3. Election of Directors
At and after the Turnover Meeting, all Directors shall be elected by a majority vote of the Members at the annual or a special meeting called for that purpose, with each Member entitled to votes per Article III, Section 2.

### Section 4. Terms of Directors
- 4.1 Directors elected at the Turnover Meeting serve until the first annual meeting.
- 4.2 At the first annual meeting, Members shall elect one Director for one year, one for two years, and one for three years.
- 4.3 Except as provided in 4.1 and 4.2, all Directors shall serve three-year terms. Any Director may serve more than one term.

### Section 5. Resignation
Any Director may resign by sending written notice to the Secretary; unless otherwise specified, resignation takes effect upon receipt by Secretary.

### Section 6. Vacancies
Vacancies caused by death or resignation shall be filled by majority vote of remaining Directors (even if less than a quorum). Such Director serves the remainder of the replaced Director's term.

### Section 7. Meetings of the Board
- 7.1 Initial Board meeting within 90 days after the Articles of Incorporation are filed.
- 7.2 All Board meetings shall be open to all Members. Except for emergency meetings, notice shall be mailed to all Members at the last address on record, **not less than 10 days before**; or posted at a place(s) on the Property at least three days prior; or by another reasonable method. Emergency meetings may be held without notice if the reason is stated in the minutes. Only emergency meetings of the Board may be conducted by telephonic communication.
- 7.3 The Board shall meet at least annually, within 30 days after each annual meeting of Members. At each annual meeting the Board shall adopt a budget and determine the regular assessment. The Treasurer shall present a financial report.
- 7.4 Special Board meetings may be called by the President or two Directors. Such meetings shall be scheduled by the Secretary within 30 days of receipt of written requests by two or more Directors. (Special procedure provided where the meeting purpose is to elect a successor Secretary or consider Secretary removal.)
- 7.5 Board meetings shall be held within Multnomah or Clackamas County, Oregon.
- 7.6 The Secretary shall give written notice to each Director **at least 10 but not more than 30 days** before each Board meeting (purpose, time, place). Notice may be waived. No Director present may object to adequacy/timeliness. If a meeting is adjourned for fewer than 30 days, no further notice is needed beyond announcement.

### Section 8. Voting by the Board
Each Director shall have one vote. So long as a quorum is present, the vote of Directors holding more than 50 percent of total votes shall be binding (unless a greater percentage is required by law or the Declaration).

### Section 9. Quorum
A majority of the Directors present in person constitutes a quorum. The Board may adjourn a meeting even if less than a quorum is present.

### Section 10. Removal
Any Director other than one appointed by Declarant may be removed, with or without cause, by affirmative vote of Members present and entitled to vote at any quorate Members' meeting. Removal must be included in the notice of the meeting. Members shall elect a replacement to serve the remainder of the term.

## ARTICLE VI — OFFICERS

### Section 1. Officers
The Officers shall be the President, the Treasurer, and the Secretary, each elected by the Board. The same person shall not concurrently hold more than one office. The Board may designate additional Officers.

### Section 2. Election and Term of Office
Officers shall be elected annually by the Board and shall hold office at the pleasure of the Board until their successors are elected and qualified. Vacancies are filled by Board election at a special meeting.

### Section 3. Removal
The Board may remove any Officer at any time, with or without cause; a successor may be elected at a special meeting.

### Section 4. Compensation
Other than out-of-pocket reimbursements, no Officer shall receive compensation unless authorized by the Board.

### Section 5. President
The President shall be a Director and the chief executive officer of the Association. Presides at Board meetings and has all general powers and duties incident to a corporation chief executive (except as otherwise provided in the Declaration).

### Section 6. Secretary
Need not be a Director or Member. Keeps minutes of all Board proceedings and Association records. Attends to giving notices required by Bylaws, Declaration, or law. Performs all other duties incident to the office at the expense of the Association.

### Section 7. Treasurer
Need not be a Director or Member. Responsible for Association funds; keeps full and accurate financial records sufficient for accounting, financial data, and tax returns. Responsible for deposits in Board-designated depositories and disbursements as permitted by Bylaws or Declaration.

## ARTICLE VII — SHARES OF STOCK AND DIVIDENDS PROHIBITED

The Association shall not have or issue shares of stock. No dividends shall be paid; no income distributed to Directors, Officers, or Members. The Association may pay reasonable compensation to Officers for services as provided in the Articles of Incorporation, the Declaration, other Bylaws provisions, or Board resolution.

## ARTICLE VIII — LOANS TO DIRECTORS AND OFFICERS PROHIBITED

### Section 1. No Loans to Directors or Officers
No loan shall be made by the Association to its Directors or Officers. Directors who vote for or assent to such a loan, and any participating Officers, shall be jointly and severally liable to the Association for the loan amount until repayment.

### Section 2. Contribution; Subrogation
Any Director against whom a claim is asserted under this Article is entitled to contribution from the other Directors who voted for the action. To the extent any Director must pay such a claim, he is subrogated to the Association's rights against the debtor on the loan.

## ARTICLE IX — CONTRACTS, CHECKS, DEPOSITS AND FUNDS

### Section 1. Contract
The Board may authorize any officer or agent to enter into contracts or execute instruments in the name of and on behalf of the Association. Such authority may be general or specific. Contracts may include employment of personnel for maintenance, upkeep, and repair of the Common Area and other property to be maintained per the Declaration.

### Section. Checks, Drafts, Etc.
All checks, vouchers, drafts, or other orders for payment, notes, or evidences of indebtedness in the Association's name shall be signed by such officer/agent and in such manner as the Board may determine.

### Section 3. Deposits
All Association funds not otherwise employed shall be deposited from time to time to the Association's credit in such banks, trust companies, or other depositories as the Board may select.

### Section 4. Gifts
The Board may accept on behalf of the Association any contribution, gift, bequest, or devise for general or special purposes.

## ARTICLE X — ARCHITECTURAL AND DESIGN CONTROL

### Section 1. Establishment of the Architectural Committee Prior to Turnover Meeting
The Architectural Committee shall consist of as many persons as Declarant may appoint. Declarant may remove any member at any time and appoint new/additional members. The Association shall keep on file the names and addresses of Committee members. Declarant may delegate to the Board the right to appoint or remove members. After delegation, the Committee shall consist of at least three persons. Once Declarant delegates to the Board (or fails to appoint by the Turnover Meeting date), the Board assumes responsibility for appointment and removal. If the Board fails to appoint, the Board shall serve as the Committee.

### Section 2. Turnover
After the Turnover Meeting, the power given the Declarant under Section 1 shall be exercised by the Board.

### Section 3. Duties and Powers
The Architectural Review Committee shall perform and is empowered to perform all acts as provided in the Declaration.

## ARTICLE XI — FINANCIAL MATTERS AND RECORDS

### Section 1. General
The Association shall keep correct and complete books and records of account, minutes of Board and committee proceedings, and at its registered or principal office a record of names and addresses of Directors. All books and records may be inspected by any Director, agent, or attorney for any proper purpose at any reasonable time.

### Section 2. Financial Statements
The Board may appoint a CPA or CPA firm as auditor (not an Officer; no interest in any Lot). Within 90 days after fiscal year end, the Board shall distribute to each Member (and any mortgagee on request) the annual financial statement (balance sheet and income/expense statement) for the preceding fiscal year. The Association shall make available to Members and to mortgage holders/insurers/guarantors, upon request during normal business hours, current copies of: (a) the Declaration, Articles of Incorporation, Bylaws, and rules; (b) the most recent financial statement; (c) the current operating budget; and (d) all other Association records. Upon written request of a prospective purchaser of a Lot, the Association shall make available these documents/items for examination and duplication during reasonable hours. The Association may charge a reasonable fee for furnishing copies.

### Section 3. Tax Return
The Board shall cause necessary income tax returns to be filed.

### Section 4. Fiscal Year
The Association's fiscal year shall commence January 1 and end December 31.

## ARTICLE XII — INSURANCE

### Section 1. Casualty and Liability Insurance
The Board shall obtain and maintain the insurance coverage required by the Oregon Planned Community Act and the Declaration. Insurance shall not be brought into contribution with insurance bought by Members or their mortgagees. Any policy obtained pursuant to the Declaration shall show the Association as named insured and, if possible, be written by an insurer with a "B" general policyholder's rating and a "III" financial size category in Best's "Key Rating Guide". Policies may contain a reasonable deductible not to exceed the lesser of $10,000 or 1% of the policy face value for fire and extended coverage; deductible amount must be added to face amount when determining whether insurance equals at least the required full replacement cost, or the policy must include a full replacement cost endorsement.

### Section 2. Director and Officer Insurance
At the Board's discretion, the Association may purchase and maintain insurance on behalf of any person who is or was a Director, Officer, employee, or agent of the Association (or who served at the Association's request) against any liability asserted against him/her, whether or not the Association would have the indemnification power under the Articles of Incorporation.

### Section 3. General Provisions
Premiums for insurance under this Article are a common expense. At least every two years, the Board shall review insurance coverage. If reasonably available, policies shall comply with the Oregon Planned Community Act and have an "inflation guard" endorsement.

## ARTICLE XIII — TRANSFER OF CONTROL

### Section 1. Transitional Committee
Within 60 days after conveyance to Members other than Declarant of a total of 50 percent of the votes of all stages of the Property (computed per Article III, Section 2(a)), Declarant shall call a special meeting of Members to select a transitional committee. Notice per Article IV, Section 3. At the meeting, Members in attendance (other than Declarant) shall, by majority of those present, select two of three transitional committee members; Declarant shall select the third. Members of the committee serve until the Turnover Meeting. Function: facilitate transition from Declarant control to Member control. If Declarant fails to call the meeting, any Member may. If Members do not select committee members, Declarant has no further obligation. Once Turnover Meeting is held, no transitional committee shall be appointed.

### Section 2. Turnover Meeting
On a date not later than 120 days after the Turnover Date, Declarant shall call the Turnover Meeting. Notice per Article IV, Section 3 to each Member, stating the purpose (relinquishment by Declarant of control), time, and place. If Declarant does not call within the required period, the transitional committee or any Member may. At the Turnover Meeting: (a) Declarant shall relinquish control and Members shall assume it; (b) then-serving Directors shall resign and Members shall elect a Board per these Bylaws.

## ARTICLE XIV — RULES AND REGULATIONS

The Board shall have power to adopt and publish rules and regulations governing the use of the Common Area and facilities, and the personal conduct of Members and their guests and tenants thereon, and to establish penalties for infractions. Rules may be adopted upon majority vote of Board members present at a quorate meeting after notice as provided in these Bylaws. Notice shall include a verbatim copy of all proposed rules and regulations. No rule shall be adopted without first delivering or mailing a copy to each Member at the last address on record. Upon adoption, copies shall be delivered to each Member. Any rule which conflicts with these Bylaws or the Declaration shall be null and void.

## ARTICLE XV — MAINTENANCE

The Association shall have the maintenance responsibilities set forth in the Declaration. The Association shall develop a program — including, but not limited to, employment of personnel — necessary for maintenance, upkeep, and repair of the Common Property and other property to be maintained per the Declaration. Costs and expenses shall be paid in the manner described in Article IX, Section 2.

## ARTICLE XVI — ASSESSMENTS

All Lots shall be subject to assessment per the Declaration to collect from Members their share of common expenses. If additional property is annexed, assessments for common expenses shall be apportioned per the Declaration.

## ARTICLE XVII — AMENDMENTS TO BYLAWS

Except as expressly provided in the Declaration, these Bylaws may be amended or repealed and new bylaws adopted by a majority of Directors present at any regular or special meeting, if at least **two days' written notice** is given of intention to amend/repeal/adopt accompanied by a copy or summary of the amendment; provided, however, that the Board shall have **no authority** to amend or repeal any Bylaws provision relating to the election, qualifications, powers, duties or terms of Directors without Member approval at a special meeting called for such purpose. **No special declarant right** (as defined in the Oregon Planned Community Act) contained in these Bylaws may be amended without Declarant's consent, and Declarant may unilaterally make Bylaws amendments permitted by the Declaration and the Oregon Planned Community Act.

## ARTICLE XVIII — WAIVER OF NOTICE

Whenever notice is required under the nonprofit corporation laws of Oregon (as exists or may be amended), or under the Articles of Incorporation or these Bylaws, a written waiver signed by the person(s) entitled to notice — whether before or after the time stated — shall be deemed equivalent to giving notice.

## ARTICLE XIX — HEADINGS

Headings in these Bylaws are for convenience and shall not affect the meaning or interpretation.

## ARTICLE XX — ACTION WITHOUT A MEETING

Any action which applicable law, the Declaration, or these Bylaws require or permit Members or the Board to take at a meeting may be taken without a meeting if a written consent setting forth the action is signed by all Members or Directors entitled to vote on the matter. Such consent has the effect of a unanimous vote and shall be filed in the minutes records.

## ARTICLE XXI — CONFLICTS

These Bylaws are intended to comply with applicable law and the Declaration. In any irreconcilable conflict, applicable law and the Declaration shall control over these Bylaws, any amendments, and any rules or regulations.
