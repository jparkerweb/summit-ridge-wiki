## Introduction

This handbook was prepared to address common questions and issues associated with living at Summitridge Townhomes HOA.

The policies and definitions in this handbook have been adapted from the Declaration and the Bylaws of Summitridge Townhomes HOA. The Declaration and Bylaws continue to be the official governing documents of the Association and the homeowners.

---

## Common Elements

All maintenance, repairs and replacement to the exterior of building structures and the Common Areas shall be made by the Association and shall be charged to all homeowners as a common expense.

### Common Areas
The following are considered Common Areas:
- Private streets, parking areas, retaining walls, curbs, gutters, utilities thereon and landscaped areas.

### Building Lots
Each unit owner shall keep his or her outdoor living area in a neat, clean and sanitary condition. Building lot owners are responsible for the maintenance and cleaning of exterior glass, doors, air conditioning units, outdoor living area and snow removal from deck, entry walkway and driveway.

### Enforcement Procedures
Should an infraction occur, the Board of Directors shall request the owner to remove the infraction and repair or return common area to original state, at the cost of the owner. Should the owner not respond to the request, the management company will be contacted and further action will be taken.

## Architecture

No improvements shall be commenced, erected, placed, altered or maintained on any building lot by an owner, until the design plans and specifications showing the nature, shape, heights, materials, colors, and proposed location of the improvement have been submitted and approved by the Architectural Committee.

Procedure for any homeowner wishing to make an addition, alteration, or improvement to the exterior of their dwelling or to Common Elements:

1. Complete an Architectural Request Form
2. Submit form to Architectural Committee
3. Architecture Committee reviews application
4. The Architecture Committee has up to **90 days** from the date complete written application is submitted to respond to the unit owner.

### Decks/Patios
- Decks & Patios shall present an attractive appearance at all times.
- Decks are not to be used as storage areas (no bicycles, tarps, garbage/recycling bins, etc.).
- BBQs, patio furniture and plants are all acceptable. All patio/table umbrellas should be in good repair.

### Antennas & Satellite Dishes
- Exterior antennas/satellite dishes shall not be permitted on any building lot except as approved by the Architectural Committee.
- No satellite dish shall exceed **24 inches** in diameter.

### Air Conditioning/Fans
- No window box air conditioners or fans that extend past the window frame are permitted.
- Central air units are authorized to be installed on the side or backside of the house where designated wiring is available. Exceptions are made through Architectural Committee review.

### Exterior Lighting Maintenance
All exterior entrance, porch and garage lights shall be maintained at all times by the homeowner.

### Landscape Lighting
Homeowners must have approval from the Architectural Committee prior to installing landscape lighting. Homeowners are responsible for maintaining installed landscape lighting.

### Season Decorations and Holiday Lighting
- Homeowners must remove holiday lighting and decorations (e.g. Easter & Halloween) **one week** following the holiday.
- Christmas lights and decorations need to be removed by the **first week of the New Year**.

### Decorative Flag/Holders
- Flags no larger than **6 feet** may be installed.
- Seasonal flags may be displayed no more than **one month** prior to a holiday or beginning of season and must be removed within **two weeks** following the holiday or end of season.

### Architecture — Enforcement Procedures
- **First Offense:** Homeowner will be requested to remove infraction. Considered a warning; remedied within 24 hours.
- **Second Offense:** Letter of violation notice; remedied within 10 days.
- **Third Offense:** Letter of violation notice and fine of **$10.00 per day**, starting on the 11th day of the infraction.

## Signage

Signage approved for display without Architectural Review:

- Refer to Mt. Park rules and regulations regarding size, style and location requirements for "Garage Sale" and "For Sale" signage. Signage to be removed within **one week** of closing of sale. Homeowner is responsible to repair any damage caused to sod by installation of signage.
- "For Rent" signage is **not** to be placed within the community. Homeowner must advertise property "For Rent" by other methods.
- All other signs erected/maintained on any Building Lot must be approved by the Architectural Review Committee for appearance and location.

### Signage — Enforcement Procedures
- **First Offense:** Homeowner requested to remove signage. Warning; remedied within 24 hours.
- **Second Offense:** Letter of violation notice; remedied within 10 days.
- **Third Offense:** Letter of violation notice and fine of **$10.00 per day** starting with the 11th day of the infraction.

## Landscaping

### Plants
Homeowners are encouraged to enhance flowerbeds with seasonal plantings at their own expense. Homeowners must, to the best of their ability, maintain the uniformity of the existing landscaping when planting their own plants. The responsibility of upkeep falls upon the homeowner to maintain all personal plantings.

All dead plantings should be removed promptly.

The landscapers will not be held liable for altering plantings planted by the homeowner (e.g., through pruning, watering, etc.).

### Flower pots, window boxes, deck pots, etc.
Homeowners may place flowers along deck, patios and entryway. Homeowner is responsible for any damage caused to common area or to the exterior of the building while installing flowers. Planters on deck or deck rails require footed pots, saucers or space under pots for ventilation to prevent accelerated rotting or damage to wood or staining of cement.

## Rubbish and Trash

All garbage and other waste must be kept in appropriate sanitary containers for proper disposal. Trash and recycling bins must be kept in the garage or out of public view, except for day of collection.

All items for trash and recycling are to be put out **no sooner than the evening prior to collection**.

### Rubbish/Trash — Enforcement Procedures
- **First Offense:** Owner to receive a written warning of violation. If conditions under the CC&R's Article VII Section 2(D) exist for a period longer than 10 days after the written notice, the offense is considered second offense.
- **Second/Consecutive Offense:** Owner to receive a written notice of violation and a fine of **$10.00 per day** until remedied.

## Noise

Owners or occupants shall exercise extreme care not to make noises which may disturb other owners, including the use of musical instruments, radios, televisions, and amplifiers.

### City of Lake Oswego Codes Section 34.10.537 — Unnecessary Noise
The following are declared examples of violations:

- The use of a motor vehicle operated in such a manner as to create loud and unnecessary noise.
- The use or operation of any device designed for sound production or reproduction (radios, TVs, musical instruments, loud speakers) between the hours of **10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.**
- The gathering of any number of persons upon premises (public or private) and the creation of excess noise from collective voices between **10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.** Excessive noise means noise plainly audible within any dwelling unit which is not the source of the sound.

For complete listing, refer to City of Lake Oswego Codes, Section 34.10.537.

### Quiet Time
**Quiet Time** will be observed between **10:00 PM and 8:00 AM**. During this time no device designed for sound production (radios, televisions, musical instruments, stereos) shall be used so that the sound is heard by neighbors or in the common area. There shall not be gatherings of any number of persons, outdoors or in common areas, that create excess noises from collective voices.

### Noise — Enforcement Procedures
The offended party should first talk with the home owner/tenant and request that the noise be stopped. If noise persists, contact the City of Lake Oswego Code Services and file a noise complaint, or, if it is an emergency, call 911.

## Pets

- All pets must be carried or kept on a leash while outside.
- No animal shall be kept, bred, or raised for commercial purposes or in unreasonable numbers.
- All pets shall be registered and inoculated as required by law.
- Pet owners shall properly clean up after their pets.
- No pet shall be permitted to cause or create a nuisance or unreasonable disturbance or noise.
- All damage created by a pet is the sole responsibility of the pet owner. Damage caused to property will be repaired by the Association and cost billed to the property owner.

### Pets — Complaint Procedure
If a pet is being offensive, the offended party should first talk with the pet owner and request correction. If the offense continues, written details should be submitted to the Managing Agent or Board of Directors explaining the offense, time, place, pet description, owner's name, address, etc.

According to City of Lake Oswego, animals at large may be taken into custody by the city and disposed of in accordance with the City ordinance for disposition of personal property or for the impoundment of dogs.

### Pets — Notices and Fines
- **First Offense:** Property owner will receive a violation notice from the Managing Agent and/or Board of Directors.
- **Second/Consecutive Offense:** Property owner will receive a violation notice and a fine of **$15.00 per occurrence**.

## Parking

To assure equitable parking and access:

### Permissible Vehicles
Vehicles that may be parked within the community include conventional passenger vehicles in good repair, currently licensed and in regular use.

### Prohibited Vehicles and Equipment
The following may not be parked in the community, except for loading/unloading:
- Trailer
- Camper
- Tent Trailer
- Boat
- Motor Homes
- Recreational Vehicles (RVs)

(See Mt. Park CC&R Article VII Section 1B.)

Loading/unloading of prohibited vehicles is permitted subject to a **24-hour time limit**.

### Garage Use / Resident and Guest Parking
Garages are to be used for vehicle, trailer, camper or boat parking and **cannot be totally converted to storage and/or living space**.

- If occupant owns one vehicle, that vehicle must be garaged except for short-term parking on the driveway.
- If occupant owns more than one vehicle, one must be garaged and the other(s) parked on the driveway. The second vehicle is to be parked in your driveway to leave parking spaces in the common area designated for guests.
- If the occupant has a third vehicle, the **Board must approve** the parking of this vehicle in the community.
- Garage Doors should remain closed except to access garage areas.

### Curb Side / Street Parking
To allow emergency vehicles and owner access to driveways, there is **no curbside / street parking** within the community.

### Vehicles in Disrepair
Vehicles in a state of disrepair are not to be abandoned or to remain parked upon any Building Lot or on the Common Area for a period longer than **48 hours**. Should any Owner fail to remove such vehicle within **5 days** after the Association posts notice on the vehicle, the Association may have the vehicle removed and charge the expense to the Owner.

### Parking — Enforcement Procedures

**Garage Use:** If you are not using your garage for vehicle parking (with exception to use of trailer, camper and/or boat parking), you may be given a notice to remedy this within **30 days**. If not completed during this period, you will be called to appear at a hearing with the board of directors and possibly fined. A fine of **$200 per month** can be levied against the owner until the violation is remedied.

**Parking:**
- **First Offense:** Violation notice attached to offending vehicle. Warning; remedied within 24 hours. If not remedied, considered a second offense.
- **Second Offense:** Violation notice attached to vehicle; remedied within 24 hours or vehicle will be towed at owner's expense. Letter from managing agent or Board of Directors sent by mail to offending owner.
- **Third Offense:** Towing of offending vehicle at owner's expense. Towing of a vehicle must be authorized by at least two people, one of which must be a board member.

If more than 30 days passes between violations, the offenses recalculate.

## Rentals

In the event your unit becomes a rental:
- Homeowners are still responsible for actions of renters and their guests.
- Homeowners are still responsible for financial obligations to HOA.
- Homeowners must provide to managing agent the emergency contact information for renter.
- Renter(s) must abide by all governing documents and the rules & regulations set forth for Summitridge Townhomes HOA.

## Board Resolution — Maintenance Responsibility (Nov 1, 2016)

### Findings (whereas)
- Pursuant to Bylaws §7.2 and §7.6, all Owners and Directors were notified; quorum present per Article V §9.
- Pursuant to Declaration Article II §3.E(1), the Association is responsible for maintenance of the exteriors of Building Structures, including repairing and replacing all exterior building surfaces.
- Pursuant to Declaration Article II §3.E(2), Owners are responsible for landscaping, decks and patios, exterior items of hardware (including doors), exterior window casements, sashes and frames, window screens, storm windows, storm doors, screen doors, walkways and driveways.
- The Declaration does not contain details about repair responsibility for areas where Owner-maintained and Association-maintained components intersect (e.g., deck-to-wall transitions or windows that need to be integrated with other exterior components).
- Some maintenance responsibilities are ambiguous.
- Board has authority to make reasonable rules per Bylaws Article XIV and ORS Chapter 94.

### Resolved
- ATTACHMENT A is adopted and incorporated by reference.
- If Association-responsibility maintenance is caused by willful or negligent acts of Owner, family, guests, or invitees, the cost shall become part of that Lot's assessment.
- If an Owner fails to meet Owner maintenance responsibilities timely, the Association may levy enforcement fines and necessary fees, continuing until the work is satisfactorily completed; charged as assessments. The Association may enter Lots to conduct maintenance, repairs, replacements, or to prevent imminent injury or damage, and may charge all costs incurred to the responsible Owner as an assessment.
- Before an Owner undertakes repair or replacement of any component that is the Owner's responsibility and located on the exterior of the building, that Owner must submit an application to the ARC. The ARC may request, among other items, evidence from a construction professional that the proposed repair will not adversely impact the weatherproofing of the building envelope. Imposing this or other requirements is solely within the discretion of the ARC.

## Board Resolution — Collection of Unpaid HOA Assessments (adopted Oct 20, 2017)

### Recitals
- The Board is charged with establishing, assessing and collecting homeowner assessments.
- Adopting a uniform and systematic procedure for collection of unpaid assessments minimizes loss of assessment revenue.
- Board has entered into an agreement ("CFE Agreement") with **Vial Fotheringham LLP ("VF")** to undertake collection and enforcement of delinquent assessments on behalf of the Association.

### ARTICLE I — Authority, Duties and Obligations Under Governing Documents & Act

- **1.1 "Declaration"** = Declaration of CC&Rs of Summitridge Townhomes, LLC, recorded December 17, 1999 as 99-225640, Multnomah County, Oregon, as amended.
- **1.2 "Bylaws"** = Bylaws of Summitridge Townhomes Home Owners Association adopted May 22, 2002, as amended.
- **1.3 "Act"** = Oregon Planned Community Act, ORS 94.550 to 94.783.
- **1.4 Specific Authority and Duties.** Article II §3 of the Declaration; Article III §3 of the Bylaws; Article XIV of the Bylaws; ORS 94.630(a) — allow the Association to adopt rules.
  - Article II §3(A)(3) and §3(D) of the Declaration authorize the Board to enforce provisions of the Declaration, Bylaws, Rules and Regulations, including action to collect unpaid assessments.
  - Article IV §8(D) of the Declaration and ORS 94.630(1)(n) authorize the Board to establish late charges for delinquent assessments.
  - Article IV, §§8(A), 8(B)(2), and 8(B)(3), and ORS 94.709(4), authorize the Board on behalf of the Association to bring suit to foreclose the lien against the lot.
  - Article IV §8(B)(3) and ORS 94.709(4) authorize the Board to bring an action for a money judgment against an Owner for damages and unpaid assessments.
  - Article IV §§8(A) and 8(D) and ORS 94.709(5) provide late charges, fines, interest, and attorney fees and costs of collection are enforceable as assessments.
- **1.5 Obligations.**
  - Article IV §1 of the Declaration: owners obligated to pay assessments.
  - Article IV §3: assessments are due and payable in advance on January 1 of each calendar year.
  - Article IV §8(B): assessment is delinquent if not paid within **30 days** of due date.
  - Article IV §§8(A) and 8(B)(2) and ORS 94.709(1): all assessments, with interest, attorney fees and costs of collection, are a continuing lien on the lot.
  - Article IV §§8(A) and 8(D) and ORS 94.630(1)(n): owners are obligated to pay reasonable fees and costs (including, without limitation, attorney fees) incurred in connection with efforts to collect delinquent and unpaid assessments — regardless of whether suit or action is commenced.

### ARTICLE II — Interest, Late Fees and Other Costs
Pursuant to §1.4 above, the following apply to delinquent and unpaid assessments:
- **2.1 Interest.** Per Declaration Article IV §8(A), interest at **10% per annum** accrues on all delinquent assessments.
- **2.2 Late Charge.** Per Declaration Article IV §8(D), any assessment delinquent (per §1.5(c)) is charged a late charge of **20% of the assessment**.
- **2.3 Other Costs.** All collection costs are imposed against the owner and due when incurred:
  - **(a) Management Company Charges.** Collection charges by the management company per its contract — reasonable mailing costs, recording fees, time spent for account maintenance, similar expenses.
  - **(b) Attorney Fees.** Any attorney fees for work performed (file intake; preparing calculations; consultations and telephone calls; legal research; drafting/preparing documents and letters; depositions; trial preparation; travel time; investigations; court appearances; analyzing the account; preparing/attending post-judgment proceedings).
  - **(c) Other Costs.** Recording fees, postage, copy costs, service costs, court costs, filing fees, paralegal fees, private investigator fees, garnishment fees, similar expenses.

### ARTICLE III — Procedure
- **3.1 Association Notice of Claim of Lien.** When an assessment is levied against a lot and owner, the Association or its agent may cause a notice of claim of lien to be recorded in the Records of Multnomah County, Oregon.
- **3.2 Association Payment Demand Letter.** When an assessment is delinquent, the Association or its agent shall send the owner a written payment demand letter ("Association Payment Demand Letter") substantially in the form set forth in Exhibit A, that includes:
  - (a) Statement of the amount due under the assessment account.
  - (b) Demand for immediate payment.
  - (c) Notice that, if the stated amount due is not paid by the turnover date, the assessment account will be turned over to VF for collection per the Collection Resolution.
  - (d) Statement that the owner is responsible for payment of all costs for collection incurred, as specified in the Collection Resolution; the costs constitute assessments against the owner and the lot.
- **3.3 Turnover of Assessment Account to VF.**
  - (a) Subject to (b), when an assessment remains unpaid after the turnover date specified in the Association Payment Demand Letter, the Association or its agent shall turn over the assessment account to VF in accordance with the CFE Agreement. VF shall proceed as provided in the VF Agreement and this Resolution.
  - (b) After turnover, all contact and communications with the owner regarding the assessment account must be with VF unless VF gives written consent otherwise.
- **3.4 Initial Notice; Recording of Notice of Claim of Lien.** When an assessment account is turned over to VF, VF shall:
  - (a) **Initial Notice.** Send a written notice of delinquent balance ("Initial Notice") to the owner.
  - (b) **Association Notice of Claim of Lien.** If a notice has not been recorded, prepare and record one in Multnomah County, Oregon.
- **3.5 Final VF Payment Demand.** If the total amount due is not paid by the date stated in the Initial Notice, or arrangement made per §3.8, VF shall send a final demand letter ("Final VF Payment Demand") demanding payment of all moneys due within **10 days**.
- **3.6 Legal Action.** If the assessment, including all costs in §2.3 above, are not paid by the date in the Final VF Payment Demand, or arrangement made per §3.8, VF, on behalf of the Association, shall initiate a lawsuit for a personal money judgment against the owner or foreclose the Association lien against the lot.
- **3.7 Execution/Enforcement of Judgment.** After VF obtains a judgment, VF shall begin collection by any one or combination of: (a) garnishing the owner's bank account; (b) garnishing the owner's wages; (c) executing a writ against the owner's real or personal property; (d) any additional methods authorized by law.
- **3.8 Payment Plans.** Payment plans proposed after the assessment account is turned over to VF must be approved by VF and the Board of Directors of the Association.
- **3.9 Payments.** After turnover, all payments must be made to VF.
- **3.10 Disbursal of Funds.** Unless otherwise agreed by VF and the Board, funds shall be disbursed to the Association in accordance with the VF Agreement that provides:
  - (a) When payments are received, they are split **50/50** between two expense categories: 50% applied to past due assessments and non-attorney related collection charges and fees; 50% applied to attorney related charges and fees.
  - (b) Each payment shall be split as in (a) until one expense category is paid in full. After one is paid in full, all remaining payments are applied to the remaining balance in the other expense category.
- **3.11 Owner Responsibility; Other Remedies.**
  - (a) **Owner Responsibility For Payment of Assessments.** Regardless of any procedure prescribed in this Resolution, it is the responsibility of the owner to ensure assessments are paid when due.
  - (b) **Owner Responsibility To Update Address.** It is the sole responsibility of the owner to notify the Association in writing of any change of owner mailing address.
  - (c) **Other Association Remedies.** Nothing in this Resolution prevents the Association from taking any other actions against an owner — including termination of utilities and preventing the owner from access to recreational or service facilities — if provided under the Declaration, Bylaws, Rules and Regulations, or the Act.
