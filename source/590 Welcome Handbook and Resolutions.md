### Introduction

This handbook was prepared to address common questions and issues associated with living at Summitridge Townhomes HOA.

The policies and definitions described in this handbook have been adapted from the Declaration and the Bylaws of Summitridge Townhomes HOA. As a reminder, the Declaration and Bylaws continue to be the official governing document of the Association and of the homeowners.

### Common Elements

All maintenance, repairs and replacement to the exterior of building structures and the Common Areas shall be made by the Association and shall be charged to all homeowners as a common expense.

#### Common Areas

The following are considered Common Areas:

- Private streets, parking areas, retaining walls, curbs, gutters, utilities thereon and landscaped areas.

#### Building Lots

Each unit owner shall keep his or her outdoor living area in a neat, clean and sanitary condition. Building lot owners are responsible for the maintenance and cleaning of exterior glass, doors, air conditioning units, outdoor living area and snow removal from deck, entry walkway and driveway.

#### Enforcement Procedures

Should an infraction occur, the Board of Directors shall request the owner to remove the infraction and repair or return common area to original state, at the cost of the owner. Should the owner not respond to the request, the management company will be contacted and further action will be taken.

### Architecture

No improvements shall be commenced, erected, placed, altered or maintained on any building lot by an owner, until the design plans and specifications showing the nature, shape, heights, materials, colors, and proposed location of the improvement have been submitted and approved by the Architectural Committee.

Any homeowner wishing to make an addition, alteration, or improvement to the exterior of their dwelling or to Common Elements must follow this procedure:

1. Complete an Architectural Request Form
2. Submit form to Architectural Committee
3. Architecture Committee reviews application
4. The Architecture Committee has up to 90 days from the date complete written application is submitted to respond to the unit owner.

#### Decks/Patios

Decks & Patios shall present an attractive appearance at all times.

Decks are not to be used as storage areas, i.e. bicycles, tarps, garbage/recycling bins, etc.

BBQ's, patio furniture and plants are all acceptable. All patio/table umbrellas should be in good repair.

#### Antennas & Satellite Dishes

Exterior antenna or satellite dishes shall not be permitted to be placed upon any building lot except as approved by the Architectural Committee. No satellite dish shall exceed 24 inches in diameter.

#### Air Conditioning/Fans

No window box air conditioners or fans that extend past the window frame are permitted to be installed.

Central air units are authorized to be installed on the side or backside of house where designated wiring is available. Exceptions are made through the Architectural Committee review.

### Architecture . . . Continued

#### Exterior Lighting Maintenance

All exterior entrance, porch and garage lights shall be maintained at all times by homeowner.

#### Landscape Lighting

Homeowners must have the approval from the Architectural Committee prior to installing landscape lighting. Homeowners will be responsible for maintaining installed landscape lighting.

#### Season Decorations and Holiday Lighting

Homeowners must remove holiday lighting and decorations (i.e. Easter & Halloween) one week following the holiday. Christmas lights and decorations need to be removed by the first week of the New Year.

#### Decorative Flag/Holders

Flags no larger than 6 feet may be installed. Seasonal flags may be displayed no more than one month prior to holiday or beginning of season and must be removed within two weeks following the holiday or end of season.

#### Enforcement Procedures (Architecture)

- **First Offense:** Homeowner will be requested to remove infraction. Violation is considered a warning and is to be remedied within 24 hours.
- **Second Offense:** Owner to receive letter of violation notice that must be remedied within 10 days.
- **Third Offense:** Owner to receive letter of violation notice and fine of $10.00 per day, starting on the 11th day of the infraction.

### Signage

Signage approved for display without Architectural Review is as follows:

Please refer to Mt. Park rules and regulations regarding size, style and location requirements for "Garage Sale" and "For Sale" signage. Signage is to be removed within one week of closing of sale. Homeowner is responsible to repair any damaged caused to sod by installation of signage.

"For Rent" signage is not to be placed within the community. Homeowner must advertise property "For Rent" by other methods.

All other signs erected or maintained on any Building Lot must be approved as to appearance and location by the Architectural Review Committee.

#### Enforcement Procedures (Signage)

- **First Offense:** Homeowner will be requested to remove signage. Violation is considered a warning and is to be remedied within 24 hours.
- **Second Offense:** Owner to receive letter of violation notice that must be remedied within 10 days.
- **Third Offense:** Owner to receive letter of violation notice and fine of $10.00 per day, starting with the 11th day of the infraction.

### Landscaping

#### Plants

Homeowners are encouraged to enhance their flowerbeds with seasonal plantings at their own expense. Homeowners must, to the best of their ability, maintain the uniformity of the existing landscaping when planting their own plants. The responsibility of the upkeep shall fall upon the homeowner to maintain all personal plantings.

All dead plantings should be removed promptly.

The landscapers will not be held liable for altering plantings, planted by the homeowner, such as through pruning, watering, etc.

#### Flower pots, window boxes, deck pots, etc.

Homeowners may place flowers along deck, patios and entryway. Homeowner is responsible for any damage caused to common area or to the exterior of building while installing flowers. Planters on deck or deck rails require footed pots, saucers or space under pots for ventilation to prevent accelerated rotting or damage to wood or staining of cement.

### Rubbish and Trash

All garbage and other waste must be kept in appropriate sanitary containers for proper disposal. Trash and recycling bins must be kept in the garage or out of public view, except for day of collection.

All items for trash and recycling are to be put out no sooner than the evening prior to collection.

#### Enforcement Procedures (Trash)

- **First Offense:** Owner to receive a written warning of violation.

  If conditions under the CC&R's Article VII Section 2 (D) exists for a period longer than 10 days after the written notice of violation is made then offense is considered second offense:

- **Second/Consecutive Offense:** Owner to receive a written notice of violation and a fine of $10.00 per day until remedied.

### Neighbor Disputes

If you become involved in a dispute with a neighbor, and are unable or uncomfortable resolving the problem directly with the neighbor, please contact the managing agent for assistance. They are experienced in handling neighbor-to-neighbor disputes and will counsel you on the proper course of action.

### Noise

Owners or occupants shall exercise extreme care not to make noises, which may disturb other owners, including the use of musical instruments, radios, televisions, and amplifiers.

**City of Lake Oswego Codes Section 34.10.537 Unnecessary Noise** states the following:

The following are declared to be examples of violations of the aforementioned City of Lake Oswego Unnecessary Noise Code:

- The use of a motor vehicle operated in such a manner as to create loud and unnecessary noise.
- The use or operation of any device designed for sound production or reproduction such as radios, television sets, musical instruments, and loud speakers, between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m.
- The gathering of any number of persons upon premises, whether public or private and the creation of excess noise from the collective voices of such persons between the hours of 10:00 p.m. and 7:00 a.m. Excessive noise means noise, which is plainly audible within any dwelling unit, which is not the source of the sound. For a complete listing of the "Unnecessary Noises" Code, please refer to the City of Lake Oswego Codes, Section 34.10.537

**Quiet Time** will be observed between the hours of 10:00 PM and 8:00 AM. During this time no device designed for sound production such as radios, televisions, musical instruments, and stereos shall be used so that the sound is heard by neighbors or in the common area.

Also, during this time, there shall not be gatherings of any number of persons, outdoors or in common areas, that create excess noises from the collective voices.

#### Enforcement Procedures (Noise)

The offended party should first talk with the home owner/tenant and request that the noise be stopped. Should the noise persist, contact the City of Lake Oswego Code Services and file a noise complaint, or if it is an emergency call 911. For contact information see above.

### Pets

All pets must be carried or kept on a leash while outside.

No animal shall be kept, bred, or raised for commercial purposes or in unreasonable numbers.

All pets shall be registered and inoculated as required by law.

Pet owners shall properly clean up after their pets.

No pet shall be permitted to cause or create a nuisance or unreasonable disturbance or noise.

All damage created by a pet is the sole responsibility of the pet owner. Damage caused to property will be repaired by the association and cost to repair will be billed to the property owner.

#### Enforcement Procedure (Pets)

##### Complaint Procedure

If a pet is being offensive, the offended party should first talk with the pet owner and request correction. If the offense continues, written details should be submitted to the Managing Agent or Board of Directors explaining the offense, time, place, pet description, owner's name, address, etc.

According to City of Lake Oswego animals at large may be taken into custody by the city and disposed of in accordance with the procedures provided by the City ordinance for disposition of personal property or for the impoundment of dogs.

##### Notices and Fines

- **First Offense:** Property owner will receive a violation notice from the Managing Agent and/or Board of Directors
- **Second/Consecutive Offense:** Property owner will receive a violation notice and a fine of $15.00 per occurrence.

### Parking

In order to assure equitable parking and access to the community the following policy has been established.

**Permissible vehicles:** Vehicles that may be parked within the community include conventional passenger vehicles in good repair and that are currently licensed and in regular use.

**Prohibited Vehicles and Equipment:** The following vehicles may not be parked in the community, except for loading or unloading of vehicle:

- Trailer
- Camper
- Tent Trailer
- Boat
- Motor Homes
- Recreational Vehicles (RV's)
- Please refer to Mt. Park C&R Article VII Section 1B

Loading or unloading of prohibited vehicles and equipment is permitted subject to a 24-hour time limit.

**Garage Use/Resident and Guest Parking:** Garages are to be used for vehicle, trailer, camper or boat parking and cannot be totally converted to storage and/or living space.

If occupant owns one vehicle, that vehicle must be garaged except for short-term parking on the driveway. If the occupant owns, more than one vehicle, one vehicle must be garaged and the other vehicle(s) parked on the driveway.

The second vehicle is to be parked in your driveway to leave parking spaces in the common area designated for guests. If the occupant has a third vehicle, the Board must approve the parking of this vehicle in the community.

Garage Doors should remain closed except to have access to garage areas.

**Curb Side / Street Parking:** To allow emergency vehicles and owners access to driveways, there is no curbside / street parking within the community.

### Parking . . . Continued

**Vehicles in Disrepair:** Vehicles in the state of disrepair are not to be abandoned or to remain parked upon any Building Lot or on the Common Area for a period longer than 48 hours. Should any Owner fail to remove such vehicle within five (5) days following the date on which the Association posts notice on the vehicle, the Association may have the vehicle removed from the Property and charge the expense of such removal to the Owner.

#### Enforcement Procedures (Parking)

##### Garage Use:

In the event that you are not using your garage for vehicle parking, with exception to the use of trailer, camper and/or boat parking, you may be given a notice to remedy this within 30 days. If not completed during this time period, you will be called to appear at a hearing with the board of directors and possibly fined. A fine of **$200 per month** can be levied against the owner until the violation is remedied.

##### Parking:

- **First Offense:** Violation notice shall be attached to offending vehicle. Violation is considered a warning and is to be remedied within 24 hours. If not remedied in this time period, the violation will then be considered a second offense (see below).
- **Second Offense:** Violation notice shall be attached to the offending vehicle. The violation must be remedied within 24 hours or the vehicle will be towed at the owner's expense. Letter from managing agent or Board of Directors to be sent by mail to offending owner.
- **Third Offense:** Towing of offending vehicle will be towed at owner's expense. Towing of a vehicle must be authorized by at least two people, one of which must be a board member.

If more than 30 days passes between the above violations, the offenses recalculate.

### Rentals

In the event that your unit becomes a rental, please remember:

- Homeowners are still responsible for action of renters and their guests
- Homeowners are still responsible for financial obligations to HOA
- Homeowners must provide to managing agent, the emergency contact information for renter
- Renter(s) must abide by all governing documents and the rules & regulations set-forth for Summitridge Townhomes HOA

### Insurance

#### Property Damage Insurance

The Association shall maintain a policy of insurance covering property damage for Common Areas.

#### Liability Insurance

The Association shall maintain comprehensive public liability insurance.

#### Fidelity Insurance

The Association shall maintain Fidelity insurance for all officers, directors, trustees and employees of the Association and all other persons handling or responsible for funds of or administered by the Association.

#### Workers Compensation Insurance

The Association shall maintain workers compensation insurance to the extent necessary to comply with any applicable laws.

#### Directors' and Officers' Liability Insurance

The Association shall maintain a policy of directors' and officers' liability insurance.

#### Insurance by Homeowners

Per Article II Section 3 Item G of the CC&R's, each owner shall be responsible for obtaining at owners expense, public liability and property damage for building lot

For more information on insurance, please refer to Article II Section 3 Item F and G of the CC&R's, pages 5 and 6.

## Part 4 — Summitridge Townhomes Home Owners Association — Resolution of the Board of Directors Regarding Repair Assessment (dated May 23, 2018)

At a meeting of the Board of Directors, held on May 8, 2018, the Board unanimously made the following findings:

WHEREAS the Association Secretary, by signing below, attests that pursuant to the Association Bylaws §7.2 and §7.6, all Owners and Directors were provided notice of the meeting and a quorum of the Board of Directors was present in person at the meeting in accordance with the Association's Bylaws Article V §9;

WHEREAS, the Building Structures in the Association require repairs to the areas that are the Association's maintenance responsibility. The total cost of the repairs will be approximately $1,134,000 (Repair Cost) which includes all of the oversight, administration, financing, legal costs, and a contingency for unforeseen conditions;

WHEREAS the Board determined that a Special Assessment would be necessary to defray this unbudgeted Repair Cost in accordance with Declaration (Amended) Article IV §4;

WHEREAS, the Board referred the matter to a vote of the owners and obtained approval of at least 2/3 of Owners present in person and by proxy at the Association meeting on October 30, 2017, as required by Declaration Article IV § 5;

WHEREAS, after obtaining approval of at least 51% of the Owners present in person and by proxy at the Association meeting on October 30, 2017, the Board obtained a loan to allow Owners the option of having lower assessments levied serially rather than making one lump-sum payment; and

WHEREAS the Board of Directors has all of the powers and duties necessary for the proper functioning of the Association pursuant to ORS 94.630 and Article V §1 of the Bylaws;

NOW THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED that the Board of Directors hereby adopts a special assessment to be allocated in accordance with the Declaration and which shall begin on June 1, 2018. Owners are personally liable for all assessments imposed against their Lots, and collection of this assessment will be conducted in accordance with the Association's current Collection Resolution and any future revisions thereto. The purpose of the assessment is to pay the Repair Cost.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that, at the option of the Owner, the Repair Assessment shall be levied in one of the following ways:

**Option 1:** One Payment of $54,000 due June 1, 2018.

**Option 2:** Serial monthly assessments of approximately $475 per month, the first of which is due June 1, 2018. The monthly assessments will continue for 15 years (180 months). The total payments under Option 2 include fees on the Association's loan, administrative costs, and also include interest. The monthly assessment amount will change to reflect adjustments in the interest rate on the Association's loan. You will be provided 30 days' notice of any change in the monthly assessment amount.

**Option 3:** One initial pre-payment of at least $1,000 due June 1, 2018. The amount of the monthly assessments will be calculated after receipt of the initial pre-payment and will be levied monthly for the next 15 years (180 months). The total payments under Option 3 include fees on the Association's loan, administrative costs, and include interest. The monthly assessment amount will change to reflect adjustments in the interest rate on the Association's loan. You will be provided 30 days' notice of any change in the monthly assessment amount.

At any time, an Owner who has selected Option 2 or 3 may prepay the entire remaining balance of the assessments without penalty, although there may be an administrative charge for calculating the payoff amount.

BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Board may adjust the amount of this Special Assessment to reflect changes to the Repair Cost. If the Special Assessment amount is reduced, the Owners who selected Option 1 and paid the Special Assessment in full before the adjustment will be entitled to a refund that will be mailed to the Owner's address on file with Superior Community Management. If ownership of the Lot changes, the prior Owner is responsible for keeping Superior Community Management advised of its current address or else any refund will be issued to the new Owner (who may not be the Owner who paid the Special Assessment).

If the Special Assessment amount is increased, the Owners who selected Option 1 and paid the Special Assessment in full before the adjustment will receive notice of an additional Special Assessment. Owners who selected Options 2 or 3 will receive notice of a higher monthly assessment for future months.

On or before June 1, 2018, the Association shall mail out a form on which the Owner shall select either Option 1, 2, or 3. Such form must be returned by June 1, 2018 to Superior Community Management along with the payment of $54,000 if Option 1 is selected. Failure to return the form by June 1, 2018 or failure to submit the payment by that date will result in the Repair Assessment being levied pursuant to Option 2.

The Board hereby directs Superior Community Management to send a copy of this resolution to every owner of record.

Dated this 23 day of May, 2018
