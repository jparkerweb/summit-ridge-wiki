## 1. Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions of SummitRidge Townhomes, LLC

**Date of Declaration:** 1999, by SummitRidge Townhomes, LLC, an Oregon limited liability company ("Declarant").
**Recorded:** Multnomah County, Oregon, December 17, 1999, Recording No. 99-225640.
**After-recording return:** City of Lake Oswego, Planning Department, 380 "A" Avenue, Lake Oswego, OR 97034.
**Executed by:** D. Michael McKenna, Managing Member, December 14, 1999 (notarized in Clackamas County by Janice Louise Benn, Oregon Notary).

### Recitals

Declarant is the owner of a subdivision in Multnomah County, Oregon, known as the SummitRidge Townhomes (the "Property"), created by subdivision plat filed for record in the Official Records of Multnomah County, Oregon (the "Plat"). Declarant desires to subject the Property to certain conditions, restrictions, and charges for the benefit of the Property and its present and subsequent owners, and to cause the power to enforce such conditions, restrictions, reservations and charges to reside in the SummitRidge Home Owners Association, an Oregon non-profit corporation.

### Declaration

The Property shall be held, sold and conveyed subject to the following easements, covenants, restrictions, and charges. Such "covenants and restrictions" shall run with the land, shall be enforceable as equitable servitudes, shall be binding on all parties having any right, title or interest in the Property, and shall inure to the benefit of each owner.

### ARTICLE I — Definitions

- **A. "Association"** means the Summit at Mountain Park Home Owners Association, its successors and assigns.
- **B. "Board"** means the Board of Directors of the Association.
- **C. "Building Site"** means the portion of a Lot upon which a Living Unit may be constructed.
- **D. "Building Structure"** means a structure comprised of one or more contiguous Living Units constructed and located on Lots, including (without limitation) garage structures located on the same Lots and attached to the Building Structure.
- **E. "City"** means the City of Lake Oswego, Oregon.
- **F. "Common Properties"** means areas of land intended for the common use, enjoyment and/or benefit of all Members, and includes the tracts designated as "Private Open Space Tracts" labeled Tracts "A" and "B".
- **G. "Improvement"** means every structure or improvement of any kind, including but not limited to a fence, wall, driveway, storage shelter, patio, deck, or other product of construction efforts on or in respect to a Lot.
- **H. "Living Unit"** means any structure on the Property designed and intended for use, occupancy and ownership as a residence by a single family.
- **I. "Lot"** means any numbered plot of land shown on the Plat, designated by Lot 1 through Lot 21 inclusive.
- **J. "Member"** means every person or entity who holds membership in the Association.
- **K. "Master Restrictions"** means the restrictive covenants imposed on the Property by Declaration recorded August 5, 1968, in Book 633, Page 882, of the Official Records of Multnomah County, Oregon, as amended.
- **L. "Outdoor Living Area"** means a portion of a Lot located immediately adjacent to a Building Structure that is screened, enclosed, or set off in any manner to create a private outdoor living/landscaped area. Outdoor Living Areas shall initially be established by Declarant at the time of construction and may be modified by the Owner with Architectural Review Committee approval.
- **M. "Owner"** means the record owner (one or more persons or entities) of title to any Lot, including fee simple owners and contract vendees, but not a mortgagee or vendor under a land sale contract who holds only a security interest.
- **N. "Plat"** has the meaning designated in the Recitals.
- **O. "Private Open Space Tracts"** means tracts of the Property so designated on the Plat.
- **P. "Property"** has the meaning designated in the Recitals.
- **Q. "Set back"** means the minimum distance between a structure and a Lot line as shown on the Plat.
- **R. "Street"** means any public right-of-way for vehicular traffic shown on the Plat.
- **S. "Street Frontage"** means that portion of a Lot or Building Site which borders on a Street.

Other terms are defined elsewhere herein.

### ARTICLE II — Membership and Voting Rights In and Powers of the Association

#### Section 1. Membership
Members shall be every Owner of a fee or undivided fee interest in any Lot and every person who holds a contract vendee's interest of record in a Lot. There shall be no other qualification for membership. Membership terminates on transfer of fee simple title or on transfer of a contract purchaser's interest by a contract vendee who qualifies as a Member. If an Owner sells a Lot by contract of sale, the Owner's membership terminates and the contract purchaser's commences upon recordation.

#### Section 2. Voting Rights
Two classes of voting membership:

**A. Class A Members** — All Members other than the Class B Member. Class A Members are entitled to one vote for each Lot in which they hold the membership-required interest. If multiple persons hold such interest in a single Lot, all are Members but only one vote may be cast per Lot. If multiple owners cannot agree on how to vote, they are deemed to have abstained. Class A Members are not entitled to elect any members of the Board so long as Class B membership exists.

**B. Class B Member** — The Declarant and any successor or assign acquiring all of Declarant's interest for purposes of completing development and selling unsold Lots. The Class B Member is entitled to three votes per Lot owned. The Class B Member is entitled to elect all Board members until Class B converts to Class A. Class B converts to Class A on the earliest of: (i) Class A Members hold votes ≥ 15; (ii) December 31, 2001; or (iii) Declarant elects to convert by written notice to the Secretary.

#### Section 3. Powers and Obligations

**A. Powers and Obligations.** The Association shall have, exercise and perform all of the following:
- **(1) Declaration.** Powers, duties and obligations granted by this Declaration.
- **(2) Statutory Powers.** Powers and obligations of a nonprofit corporation under the Oregon Nonprofit Corporation Act and of a homeowners association of a planned community under the Oregon Planned Community Act.
- **(3) General.** Any additional or different powers, duties and obligations necessary or desirable to effectuate any right or privilege given herein for the purpose of carrying out the functions of the Association or otherwise promoting the general benefit of Owners.

The powers and obligations may be amended, repealed, enlarged or restricted by changes in this Declaration in accordance with the provisions herein, accompanied by changes in the Articles or Bylaws and the Oregon Nonprofit Corporation Act.

**B. Liability.** Neither the Association nor any officer or member of the Board shall be liable to any Owner for any damage, loss or prejudice suffered or claimed on account of any action or failure to act, provided only that the officer or Board member acted in good faith in accordance with the actual knowledge possessed by him.

**C. Association Rules and Regulations.** The Association may adopt, modify, or revoke rules and regulations governing the conduct of persons and the operation and use of Lots and the Common Area and Landscaped Areas, as deemed necessary or appropriate to assure peaceful and orderly use and enjoyment. Any rule or amendment affecting Tracts A and B shall be subject to written approval by the City. Rules shall be delivered by the Board promptly to each Owner and shall be binding upon all Owners and occupants on the date of delivery. The method of adoption shall be as provided in the Bylaws.

**D. Similar Duties of the Association.** Without limitation, the Association shall conduct and perform the following duties, funded by assessments:

**E. Maintenance of Building Exteriors, Common Area, and Landscaped Areas.**

**(1) By the Association.** The Association shall be responsible for maintenance of the exteriors of all Building Structures and maintenance and repair of the Common Area (including any utilities thereon, to the extent not maintained by governmental authorities). Maintenance of the exteriors of Building Structures shall include painting, staining, re-staining, repairing, and replacing of all exterior surfaces, including roofs (but excluding repair and replacing of exterior doors); painting and staining of exterior window casements, sashes, frames, window screens, storm windows, storm doors, and screen doors; maintaining, repairing and replacing exterior lighting fixtures, the exterior portions of chimneys, rain gutters, down spouts, and sprinkler timing devices; and cleaning the exterior surfaces of skylights. Maintenance of the Common Area and exterior of all Building Structures shall include repairing and replacing grass, sod, trees, shrubs, and bushes in a neat, clean, and attractive condition, as well as the maintenance and repair of all underground sewer systems. **Tract A is intended to remain in its natural condition** and any action that would or will disturb its natural condition shall be subject to prior written approval by the City. The Association also has authority (but is not required) to arrange for snow and ice removal from the Common Area (except Tract A shall not be disturbed), but not from the Lots. Decisions as to the nature, extent and timing of maintenance for a particular Building Structure are solely within the Board's discretion.

**(2) By the Owners.** The Association's maintenance does not include the following — Owner responsibilities: repairing, replacing, restoring, or cleaning of (i) glass (other than cleaning exterior surfaces of skylights) and (ii) landscaping and other improvements (including, without limitation, decks and patios) located on a Lot and/or within an Outdoor Living Area; exterior items of hardware (including replacing/repairing exterior doors); exterior window casements, sashes and frames (other than painting/staining); window screens, storm windows, storm doors, or screen doors (other than painting/staining); walkways and driveways; electrical and mechanical doorbells and knockers; air conditioning and heating equipment and devices. Owners are also responsible for maintaining, repairing, and replacing dwelling unit interiors within Building Structures, including without limitation: electrical wiring and fixtures, plumbing pipes and conduits, all fixtures and appliances (built-in or freestanding), air conditioning, heating, sewage disposal, and interior fire protection systems and all amenities and hardware located within Building Structure interiors. Each Owner shall also be responsible for snow and ice removal from that Owner's Lot.

**F. Insurance.**

**(1) By the Association.** The Association shall obtain and maintain in effect, from reputable insurance companies authorized in Oregon, public liability and property damage insurance with respect to all Common Area, in such amounts and forms as the Board deems advisable to provide adequate protection for bodily injury (including deaths) and property damage, whether caused by negligence of the Association or otherwise. Coverage shall not be less than **$1,000,000.00 per person, per occurrence**. Policies shall provide that coverage cannot be canceled or substantially modified without at least 10 days' written notice to the Association. The Association shall also obtain and maintain fire and extended coverage casualty insurance (including damage from vandalism and malicious mischief) with respect to each Building Structure and the Common Area (including any insurable Improvements thereon) in an amount equal to **100% of replacement cost**. The casualty coverage may be on a "blanket" basis. The Association may obtain other insurance as it deems advisable. Casualty insurance shall include the following terms if reasonably available: (a) waiver of subrogation by the insurer as to any claims against the Board, any Owner, or any guest; and (b) waiver by the insurer of its right to repair and reconstruct instead of paying cash.

**G. By the Owners.** Each Owner shall obtain and maintain, from reputable insurance companies authorized in Oregon, public liability and property damage insurance with respect to such Lot in an amount of not less than **$500,000.00 per person, per occurrence**. Each Owner shall also obtain and maintain fire and extended coverage casualty insurance (including coverage for vandalism and malicious mischief) with respect to all insurable Improvements located on such Lot, other than the Building Structure thereof, in an amount equal to **100% of the replacement cost**. Each Owner shall also be responsible for obtaining fire and extended coverage casualty insurance for the Owner's personal property. No Owner shall obtain any of the insurance coverages described in Section 3(F), nor shall any Owner-obtained (or such Owner's mortgagee-obtained) insurance be brought into contribution with insurance obtained by the Association.

### ARTICLE III — The Common Properties

#### Section 1. Members' Easements of Enjoyment
Subject to Section 3, every Member shall have an easement and right of enjoyment in and to the Common Properties; such easement is appurtenant to and shall pass with the title to every Lot upon recordation of a contract of sale of any Lot.

#### Section 2. Title to Common Properties
The Declarant may retain legal title to the Common Properties until improvements thereon are completed and the Association is able to maintain them in Declarant's judgment. Notwithstanding, the Declarant **shall convey** the Common Properties to the Association no later than December 31, 2001, if not sooner conveyed.

#### Section 3. Extent of Members' Easements
The rights and easements of enjoyment shall be subject to the following:

A. The right of the Association to limit the number of guests of Members.
B. The right of the Association to suspend the enjoyment rights of any Member with respect to any Common Property for any period during which any assessment remains unpaid, and for any period not to exceed thirty days for any infraction of published rules and regulations.
C. The right of the Declarant and the Association to mortgage the Common Properties as security for any loan, the purpose of which is improvement of the Common Properties or any Lot, provided any such mortgage is subordinate to this Declaration and the Plat.
D. The right of the Association to dedicate or transfer all or any part of the Common Properties to any municipal corporation, public agency or authority for such uses and purposes and subject to such conditions as agreed by Members, subject to written approval of the City; no such dedication or transfer shall be made except upon a vote of approval of Members per the Articles and approval in writing by the City.
E. The right of the Declarant to grant easements encumbering the Common Properties to any public agency, authority or utility as required to develop the Property as a single family subdivision, provided trees within the Private Open Space Tracts are fully protected, subject to review and written approval by the City. If trees on the Tracts are damaged, the Declarant shall replace them as requested by the City.
F. Reasonable rules and regulations regarding use of the Common Properties may be adopted by the Declarant or the Association (Declarant to adopt initial rules; thereafter the Association acting through the Board may modify), subject to City approval.
G. The Private Open Space Tracts may be used only for **passive recreational purposes** such as walking and picnicking; no other use may be made of the Private Open Space.

#### Section 4. Maintenance of Common Properties
The Association shall be responsible for maintaining and repairing the Common Properties, including the Private Open Space Tracts; maintenance and repairs to be funded by assessments collected hereunder. The City shall have the right to enforce these maintenance and repair obligations at the expense of the Association and Owners.

### ARTICLE IV — Covenant for Assessments

#### Section 1. Creation of the Lien and Personal Obligation of Assessment
Declarant covenants for each Lot owned by it, and each Owner of any Lot, by acceptance of the deed or contract — whether or not so expressed — shall be deemed to covenant and agree to pay to the Association annual assessments and special assessments for capital improvements. Such assessments shall be fixed, established and collected as provided. Annual and special assessments, together with interest, costs of collection (and such costs as further provided), shall be a continuing lien upon the Lot against which each assessment is made. Each assessment, together with interest and costs of collection, shall also be a personal obligation of the person who is the Owner at the time the assessment fell due.

#### Section 2. Purpose of Assessments
Assessments shall be used exclusively for the improvement and maintenance of the Common Properties, for the general purposes of promoting the recreation, health, safety, welfare, common benefit and enjoyment of the Owners, performance of Association obligations under this Declaration, and for the satisfaction of any obligations of the Association under the Parking Agreement, all as more specifically authorized from time to time by the Board.

#### Section 3. Annual Assessment
The Board shall prepare a budget covering the estimated costs of operating the Association each calendar year, which may include a reserve in accordance with a capital budget separately prepared. The Board shall cause the budget and the assessment to be levied against each Lot for the following year, delivered to each owner at least **thirty (30) days** prior to the end of the current calendar year. The budget and the assessment shall become effective unless disapproved at a meeting of the Owners by a majority of the Members entitled to vote. If Members disapprove or the Board fails to determine the budget for a calendar year, the budget in effect for the prior year continues for the succeeding year.

#### Section 4. Special Assessments for Capital Improvements
*(See First Amendment, recorded 2016, which amends this section, and Second Amendment 2016 wording above.)*
Upon vote of the Members in the manner set forth, the Association may levy in addition to annual assessments a special assessment in any calendar year applicable to that year only, for the purpose of defraying in whole or in part the cost of construction or reconstruction or expected repair or replacement of a described capital improvement upon the Common Properties, including necessary fixtures and personal property related thereto.

*[As amended September 2, 2016 — see First Amendment below — Section 4 reads: "Upon vote of the Members ... the Association may levy a special assessment for the purpose of defraying in whole or in part the cost of construction or reconstruction or expected repair or replacement of any component for which the Association is responsible under this Declaration, including necessary fixtures and personal property related thereto."]*

#### Section 5. Voting and Notices for Special Assessment
Any special assessment must have the assent of **two-thirds of the votes of each class of Members** voting in person or by proxy at a duly called meeting, with written notice sent to all Members at least 30 days in advance setting forth the purpose.

#### Section 6. Date of Commencement of Annual Assessment
The initial annual assessment shall commence on a date determined by the Board and be due and payable on the date fixed by the Board. Annual assessments for any year after the first shall become due and payable on the first day of such year. The amount of the initial annual assessment for the first year, or for any property which becomes subject to assessment for the first time, shall be prorated on a calendar year basis according to the date of subjection. The due date of any special assessment shall be fixed in the resolution authorizing it.

#### Section 7. Notice of Assessments
At least 30 days in advance of the due date, the Board shall fix the amount of the annual assessment against each Lot and give the owners written notice. Upon demand at any time, the Association shall furnish to any owner liable for an assessment a certificate in writing indicating whether the assessments on the Lot owned by such Owner have been paid.

#### Section 8. The Effect of Non-Payment of Assessments; Lien of Association

**A.** *(As amended October 2017 — see Second Amendment 2017.)* If an assessment is not paid on the due date, such assessment shall become delinquent and shall bear interest at the rate of **10% per annum** from such due date. If so directed by the Board, the secretary of the Association may file in the office of the Multnomah County Clerk within 90 days after delinquency a statement of the amount of the delinquent assessments together with interest and notice of the lien of the Association securing such obligation. Upon payment in full, a release shall be executed and filed. Such assessment with interest as set forth above, **together with all costs and attorney fees incurred by the Association in connection with the collection of the delinquency, whether or not suit is filed**, shall constitute a Lien on the applicable Lot from the date of filing notice of delinquency until released, and shall constitute a personal obligation of the Owner. The Association may bring an action at law to enforce payment of a delinquent assessment against the Owner personally obligated to pay the same and may enforce such lien in the manner provided by law with respect to a lien on real property.

No Owner may waive or otherwise escape liability for assessments by non-use of the Common Properties or abandonment of his Lot.

**B. Default in Payment of Assessments; Enforcement of Lien.** If an Assessment or other charge levied under this Declaration is not paid within 30 days after its due date, such Assessment or charge shall become delinquent and shall bear interest from the due date until paid at the rate set forth below; and, in addition, the Association may exercise any or all of the following remedies:

- **(1) Suspension of Rights; Acceleration.** The Association may suspend such Owner's voting rights and right to use the Common Area until such amounts, plus other charges under this Declaration, are paid in full and may declare all remaining periodic installments of any Assessment or other amounts owed by such Owner immediately due and payable. In no event, however, shall the Association deprive any Owner of access to and from such Owner's Lot.
- **(2) Lien.** The Association shall have a lien against each Lot for any Assessment levied and any fines or other charges imposed under this Declaration or the Bylaws against the Owner from the date the Assessment, fine or charge is due. The provisions regarding attachment, notice, recordation and duration of liens established on real property under ORS 87.352 to 87.382 shall apply to the Association's lien. The lien shall be foreclosed in accordance with the foreclosure of liens under ORS Chapter 88. The Association, through its duly authorized agents, may bid on the Lot at such foreclosure sale, and may acquire and hold, lease, mortgage and convey the Lot. If any Assessment is payable in installments, the full amount of the Assessment is a lien from the date the first installment becomes due.
- **(3) Suit or Action.** *(As amended October 2017 — see Second Amendment 2017.)* The Association may bring an action to recover a money judgment for unpaid Assessments, fines and charges under this Declaration without foreclosing or waiving the lien described in this Article. Recovery of funds on any such action, however, shall operate to satisfy the lien, or the portion thereof, for which recovery of funds is made.
- **(4) Other Remedies.** The Association shall have any other remedy available to it by law or in equity.

**C. Notification of First Mortgagee.** The Board shall notify any first mortgagee of any Lot of any default in performance of the terms of this Declaration by the Lot Owner which is not cured within 60 days.

**D. Interest, Expenses and Attorneys' Fees.** *(As amended October 2017 — see Second Amendment 2017.)* Any amount not paid to the Association when due shall bear interest from the due date until paid at a rate of **10 percent per annum**. A late charge may be charged for each delinquent assessment in an amount established from time to time by resolution of the Board not to exceed **30%** of such assessment. In the event the Association files a notice of lien, the lien amount shall also include all costs and attorney fees incurred by the Association in connection with the collection of the delinquency, whether or not suit is filed. In all cases, the Association shall be entitled to recover from an Owner all costs and expenses, including attorney fees, incurred in connection with the collection of money due or the enforcement of this Declaration.

#### Section 9. Subordination of the Lien to Mortgages
The lien of assessments provided herein shall be **subordinate to the lien of any mortgage or deed of trust** encumbering a Lot. Sale or transfer of any Lot shall not affect the assessment lien. However, sale or transfer of any Lot subject to such mortgage/deed of trust pursuant to a decree of foreclosure (or proceeding in lieu, including sale under a deed of trust) shall extinguish any lien for an assessment that became a lien prior to such sale or transfer. Such sale shall not release the Lot from liability for assessments thereafter due, or from the lien thereof.

#### Section 10. Capitalization of Association
Upon acquisition of record title to a Lot from Declarant, the purchasing owner shall contribute to the capital of the Association **$300.00**. This payment shall be deposited by the purchasing owner in escrow and disbursed from escrow to the Association, unless Declarant previously paid the assessment for the Lot in question — in which event the contribution shall be delivered to Declarant.

#### Section 11. Payment of Assessments by Declarant
After commencement of annual assessments, the Declarant shall pay **50%** of the annual assessment due for Lots owned by Declarant upon which a single family dwelling has been completed and **10%** of the annual assessment due for Lots owned by Declarant which are not improved with a single-family dwelling. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, the Declarant may contribute assessments due in services or materials or a combination of services and materials, rather than money (an "In-kind Contribution"). The amount by which monetary assessments shall be decreased as a result of any In-kind Contribution shall be the fair market value of the contribution. If the Declarant and the Association agree on the value, that shall be the value. If they cannot agree, the Declarant shall supply the Association with a detailed explanation of services performed/materials furnished, and the Association shall acquire bids from three independent contractors approved by the Declarant who are in the business of providing such services and materials. If still unable to agree, the value shall be deemed the average of the bids received from the independent contractors.

### ARTICLE V — Restrictions on Use of Property by Occupants

#### Section 1. Use Restrictions

A. **No Lot shall be used for any purpose other than single-family residential purposes.**
B. **No animals or fowl** shall be raised, kept or permitted upon the Property or any part thereof, excepting only domestic dogs or cats and excepting caged birds kept within the dwelling house, provided (i) said dogs, cats, and pet birds are not permitted to run at large and are not kept, bred, or raised for commercial purposes, and (ii) **no more than one (1) of any such pets** shall be permitted to be kept on any Lot.
C. No part of the Property shall be used for the purposes of exploring for, taking or producing **gas, oil or other hydrocarbons or minerals**.
D. No noxious or offensive activity shall be carried on upon the Property or any part thereof, nor shall anything be done or maintained thereon which may be or become an annoyance or nuisance to the neighborhood or detract from its value as a residential district.
E. It shall be the duty of each owner or occupant of any Lot to improve and maintain in proper condition the area between the property line of said Lot and the nearest curb or improved street, and **no trucks, campers, trailers, or boats shall be parked or permitted to remain in said area**.
F. The **Private Open Space Tracts shall remain in their natural condition** for the purpose of providing a scenic, aesthetic appearance, protecting natural processes, providing passive recreational uses, and maintaining natural vegetation. Trees may be removed from the Tracts only after they have been shown to be hazardous to life or property by a certified arborist, and after a tree cutting permit has been obtained from the City. Improvements (including public or private utilities) which are in keeping with the above purpose, must be approved by the City. **No buildings shall be allowed in the Private Open Space Tracts.**
G. No Lot shall be used or maintained as a dumping ground for **rubbish, garbage or trash**. Garbage and other waste shall be kept in sanitary containers. All incinerators or other equipment for storage or disposal shall be kept in a clean and sanitary condition, and comply with all local, state and Federal requirements.
H. **Trees** planted along the streets and within the public right-of-way may be removed at the discretion of the governmental body. All other trees shall be preserved and may only be removed after they have been shown to be hazardous by a certified arborist, and after a tree cutting permit has been obtained from the City. A detailed landscape plan shall be submitted along with any tree cutting application for review and approval by the City. The City may recommend revisions to preserve the treed character of the neighborhood.
I. The **utility easements and setbacks** applicable to the Property, if any, are reflected on the Plat and incorporated herein by reference. Where utilities are constructed on side or rear Lot lines, removal/replacement of fences for public access to such utilities shall be at the expense of the owner of the applicable Lot.
J. **No parking** of automobiles, boats, trucks, trailers or other vehicles shall be permitted on the Streets.
K. The Lots and Common Property are subject to the **Tree Code of the City of Lake Oswego**, and no trees may be removed without complying with such Tree Code and this Article.
L. **Disabled vehicles** shall not be permitted to remain on any Lot for more than three (3) consecutive days unless situated within an enclosed garage.

### ARTICLE VI — Restrictions on Type of Structures Permitted

#### Section 1. Restrictions on Structures
In addition to requirements imposed by any municipal corporation having jurisdiction over the Property:

A. Except with the approval of the Association, **no building may be erected or maintained on any Lot except one single family dwelling** designed for occupancy by not more than one family, together with a private garage, which garage shall conform generally in architectural design and exterior materials and finish to the dwelling house to which it is appurtenant. **No outhouse of any kind, tent, shed or trailer**, or any other temporary dwelling, shall be erected or maintained on any Lot or be used for living purposes, nor shall any garage be used for dwelling purposes. **Garden sheds or tool sheds may not be erected.**
B. **No trailer, camper, or pickup coach, tent, boat or truck (except pickup) shall be parked, placed, erected, maintained or constructed on any Lot for any purpose.** However, trailers, campers, pickup coaches, tents or boats which can be and are stored completely within a garage and are not used for living purposes will not be in violation.
C. No building shall be in any manner occupied while in the course of original construction or until it complies with all requirements as to area and with all other conditions and restrictions applicable thereto. Construction shall be prosecuted with reasonable diligence continuously from time of commencement until fully completed. **No buildings constructed elsewhere shall be moved to or placed on the Property** except with the written approval of the Association.
D. The Property is subject to the **Master Restrictions** and any construction undertaken on the Lots must comply with the Master Restrictions.

### ARTICLE VII — Restrictions Regarding Construction, Maintenance and Use

#### Section 1. Architectural Committee
Declarant shall appoint an Architectural Committee (the "AC") of three or more persons (need not be Members), which Committee may act for the Board and Declarant to the extent set forth in this Declaration. Declarant may remove or replace AC members at will until Declarant transfers AC selection authority to the Board. Authority to designate AC members shall be transferred to the Board upon the earlier of: (i) Declarant elects to transfer by written notice; or (ii) closing of the sale of all Lots to third parties by Declarant.

**No Improvement** shall be commenced, erected or maintained upon the Property, **nor shall any exterior addition to or change or alteration therein be made until the plans and specifications** showing the nature, kind, shape, height, color, materials, and location of the same have been submitted to and approved in writing by the AC. If the AC fails to approve or disapprove within **ninety days** after submission, approval will not be required and this Article will be deemed fully complied with.

#### Section 2. Restrictions on Construction, Maintenance and Improvement

**A. Structures Permitted.** Except as expressly provided or contemplated, no Improvements shall be erected or permitted on any Lot except Improvements designed for single family residential living and Improvements normally accessory thereto. All Improvements must be approved by the AC prior to construction.

**B. Residential Use.** Lots shall only be used for residential purposes. Except with the consent of the Board and as allowed by applicable ordinances, agreements, or land use approvals, **no trade, business, profession, commercial or similar activity** of any kind shall be conducted on any Lot, nor shall any goods, equipment, vehicles, materials or supplies used in connection with any trade, service or business be kept or stored on any Lot. Nothing prohibits: (i) activities relating to rental or sale of Building Structures; (ii) the right of Declarant or any contractor or homebuilder to construct Building Structures, store construction materials/equipment on Lots in the normal course of construction, and use any Building Structure as a sales or rental office or model home; (iii) the right of an Owner to maintain a personal professional library, keep personal business records or accounts, handle personal business or professional telephone calls or confer with business or professional associates, clients or customers, in his Building Structure. The Board shall not approve commercial activities otherwise prohibited unless the Board determines only normal residential activities would be observable outside the Building Structure and activities would not violate applicable ordinances.

**C. Signs.** No signs shall be erected or maintained on any Lot, except signs approved as to appearance and location by the AC. Restrictions do not apply to the temporary placement of "**political**" signs on any Lot by the Owner thereof.

**D. Rubbish and Trash.** No Lot or part of the Common Area shall be used as a dumping ground for trash or rubbish. All garbage and waste shall be kept in appropriate sanitary containers for proper disposal and out of public view. ## 2. Exhibit A — Wetlands Maintenance Plan

**Title:** Maintenance Plan for Summit Ridge Townhome Planned Unit Development Private Tracts and Wetland Area, City of Lake Oswego, Oregon
**Date:** December 7, 1999

### Introduction
SummitRidge Townhomes LLC is developing a planned unit community of 21 attached single family homes along Summit Ridge Court. The site contains a designated wetland area consisting of two distinct small wetlands separated by a narrow buffer area (Tract A) and an open area with a storm water detention pond (Tract B). The wetland is defined on the landscaping plan on file with the City of Lake Oswego and the initial planting will be completed per that plan. It is required that the SummitRidge Homeowners Owners Association assume responsibility for maintenance of the wetlands and surrounding buffer areas. The landscape plan for SummitRidge Townhomes PUD provides a specific plan for planting in the buffer area; the Developer is required to complete that plan prior to obtaining final occupancy permits. The HOA's requirement is to maintain the landscape.

### Initial Maintenance
The initial maintenance period will include the first growing season and will require monthly weeding, spraying, tightening (if required) and repairing guys, pruning and resetting plant material to grade and position, and when necessary replacing dead material. The installation of designated planting in the wetlands buffer area and clearing of invasive plant material from the fringe area of the wetlands will be completed by the Developer. Initial planting and first year maintenance shall require irrigation as called for in the landscaping plan; however, the initial period of growth may require additional irrigation. The removal of any invasive plants will be completed by a licensed landscape contractor, using hand work and light equipment so as not to disturb (as much as possible) the existing plant material. The most significant invasive plants are **Himalayan blackberry and English Ivy**. The preferred control is to remove the vines by hand and dig the roots as much as possible. Light applications of **RODEO® herbicide** can be applied as required to control the blackberry, using a combination of hand-held sprayer and wiper applications. Herbicide should be used sparingly and with care not to damage surrounding plant material. If removal of invasive plants in an area over **10 square feet** is necessary, replanting will be accomplished with native species approved in the initial landscaping plan after submitting a revised landscaping plan to the City for approval.

### Long Term Maintenance
Long-term maintenance will be the responsibility of the Homeowners Association. The long-term plan is to protect the wetlands, buffer areas and open space tracts from invasive plant material and maintain the wetlands in their natural condition. The buffer areas must be maintained as planted per the landscape plan for this subdivision except as modified by changes approved in writing by the City of Lake Oswego. The purpose of the buffer areas is to bridge the area between roads, sidewalks and lawn, and the wetlands itself. Control of invasive species, primarily the Himalayan blackberry and English Ivy, and the health and growth of the planted shrubs are the key ingredients of the maintenance plan for the buffer areas. Hand work and sparing use of herbicides will accomplish control of the invasive plant materials.

Inspection of the buffer areas and fringe areas of the wetlands should be conducted **semiannually** and corrective remedial action taken within a reasonable time. Diseased or dead plant material in the buffer area will be replaced with identical material unless approval for a substitute plant material is obtained in advance from the City of Lake Oswego.

### Observations
1. Some cracking of the EIFS lamina was observed at certain window corners — generally caused by stresses common to such transitions. Extent was limited but could allow water migration if unsealed; routine maintenance to seal/repair before they worsen.
2. Adjacent windows in the EIFS-clad walls have a wood trim running along the jambs between the two units. The trim is not properly flashed or sealed and has started to cup and split (more severe on weather-exposed sides), leaving open gaps for water intrusion behind underlying cladding. Contributed to the documented water damage at Exploratory Opening 7.
3. A number of sealant joints around windows in EIFS-clad walls were split and failed. Failed sealant provides a path for water leakage; should be repaired as routine maintenance.
4. EIFS was installed with grooved EPS foam panels applied over asphalt-impregnated building paper used as the weather-resistive barrier. Grooves should permit some positive drainage between EIFS foam and WRB; however, focused damage to underlying gypsum sheathing was observed near entry points behind cladding (Exploratory Openings 6 and 7), indicating water gets behind the EIFS faster than it can drain or dry.
5. Siding/trim/flashing assemblies around windows in fiber-cement walls are prone to leakage. Lacks proper flashing or weather-sealing. The wood trim used around the windows is not highly resistant to decay and can readily deteriorate when exposed to too much water. Extensive deterioration to backside of trim where water leaked behind and became trapped, holding water against the underlying WRB. The WRB is only water-resistant, not waterproof; moisture penetrates through to gypsum sheathing. Elevated moisture readings (non-destructive meter) under many windows, particularly south and west sides.
6. Small wall penetrations through lap siding (exhaust vents, crawlspace vents, fireplace direct vents, utility meters, electrical outlets, pipes, etc.) did not appear properly flashed or sealed. Water damage to gypsum sheathing beneath a fireplace direct vent at Exploratory Opening 1; water damage around an improperly sealed pipe at Exploratory Opening 2.
7. Wood-framed balcony assemblies on the west elevation are not properly flashed or sealed where they connect back to the exterior wall assemblies. Water intrusion and resultant damage at Exploratory Openings 2 and 3. Adjacent assemblies (transitions at foundations and building corners) were also problematic from a weatherproofing standpoint.
8. Swing doors and wall assemblies around second-level Juliet balconies may also be prone to leakage. Although no opening was performed there, the detailing reflects similar performance issues seen elsewhere. Exposed wood swing doors fare poorly in resisting rainwater. Adjacent cladding is a mixture of wood trim and fiber-cement paneling; fiber-cement paneling tends to hold water that migrates behind it for extended periods due to inherent lack of drainage and drying when installed directly over building papers.
9. At one location between Unit 10 and Unit 12, a section of wall along the roof edge was not properly flashed or protected from the weather. Exposed gypsum sheathing was visible, and water damaged.
10. Downspouts from the upper roof spill water directly onto the shingles of the lower roofs. Long-term, plumbing the upper downspouts directly into the gutters below would reduce roof shingle wear.
11. Paper-faced gypsum sheathing was used to sheath the exterior wall framing for fire-resistivity purposes. Paper-faced gypsum sheathing is very sensitive to moisture; both the paper facing and the food-based adhesives between facing and gypsum core deteriorate when exposed to excessive moisture, supporting organic growth. Where leakage is occurring, organic growth on the sheathing was generally present. Once wetted, gypsum loses structural integrity even when dry. De-bonded paper, fractured gypsum core, and corroded fasteners were documented at the various openings.
12. No specific means was provided to control air leakage through the exterior wall assemblies. Uncontrolled air flow can increase energy consumption and carry pollutants (soot, dust, pollen, mold spores) into the interiors and contaminate batt insulation. Warm, moisture-laden interior air can be deposited as condensation on colder surfaces in winter. Suggest steps to reduce uncontrolled air leakage whenever significant exterior wall envelope repairs are performed.

### Discussion
The exterior wall assemblies are clad in what is commonly referred to as a "**concealed-barrier**" system. Concealed-barrier systems incorporate a water-resistant material (typically building paper or housewrap) behind the exterior cladding. Small cavities behind the cladding formed within the grooved EPS foam of the EIFS and the siding laps of the fiber-cement lap siding allow some drainage of water, provide a capillary break, and enhance drying with air movement that can occur between the backside of the cladding and the WRB.

The success of these wall systems in resisting exterior water penetration depends on:
- Controlling the volume of water that penetrates the siding system,
- Correct lapping of the WRB and flashing to direct water down and out of the wall assembly, and
- The size and effectiveness of the drainage plane behind the siding to shed water down to the flashing and back to the exterior.

In moderate-to-high exposure conditions, recent history has shown that siding systems applied directly over the WRB do not provide adequate drainage and drying capacity; this type of assembly incurs a higher risk of failure in climates with extended wetting seasons such as the **Pacific Northwest**.

The amount of water that penetrates past the cladding via leakage paths at interfaces with adjacent elements often exceeds the drainage and drying capacity of the wall assemblies. Water gets trapped between siding and WRB for extended periods. Because the WRB is only water-resistant (not waterproof), moisture often penetrates to the underlying sheathing and framing, causing deterioration of hidden structural components.

Problems with water intrusion are exacerbated by improper interface details. Reliance on the exterior surface of the wall cladding and associated sealants to prevent all water penetration is **not achievable** in moderate-to-high rainfall exposure. These wall systems are appropriate only for sheltered locations or very dry climates.

Even though the wall assemblies at Summit Ridge Townhomes incorporate some drainage capability behind the cladding, it is still limited; attention to detailing of the WRB and associated flashing elements was marginal. This makes the walls more vulnerable to water that migrates past the outer cladding.

Overall, walls with the fiber-cement lap siding have less drainage capability than walls with the EIFS cladding. Fiber-cement siding walls are more likely to allow greater volumes of water to migrate behind cladding due to the multi-jointed nature of the siding, particularly at interfaces with trim, penetrating details and within siding joints themselves.

By comparison, the EIFS is more of a "monolithic" or "uniform" cladding system; as such, it is inherently better able to resist rainwater penetration as a result. The EIFS at Summit Ridge is most prone to water leakage at interfaces to dissimilar materials such as window frames and wood trims where there are discontinuities in the flashing and weather seals.

A major detractor to overall performance is the use of **paper-faced gypsum sheathing**, which has been phased out over the last decade due to moisture sensitivity. With a more durable product, the underlying impact would have been less.

During the assessment, evidence of water leakage was found at each exploratory opening, but openings were limited to areas more prone to wind-driven rain (typically from the southwest). North and east elevations appeared to be faring much better. This creates the potential to focus repairs to areas with the greatest exposure to weather.

### Conclusions and Recommendations
After about 15 years of service, the exterior wall assemblies at Summit Ridge Townhomes have experienced moderate damage from rainwater leakage past building envelope assemblies. Some level of repair is recommended to preserve and extend service life. There appear to be options to focus resources on areas of greatest risk.

Primary areas of concern for the wall assemblies:
1. Window and flashing assemblies within the lap-sided walls.
2. Balcony connections and surrounding wall assemblies.
3. Windows within the EIFS-clad walls.
4. Various small wall penetrations through the lap siding.

Many similarly constructed buildings in the region have experienced significant performance issues due to water leakage, often leading to extensive repairs or full rehabilitation well before a 30-year period (typical residential timeframe). Service-life timeframes of 50 years or more can be achieved for building envelope assemblies, but only with superior construction practices and materials. That is **not the case here**.

Based on current condition and performance to date, some significant repairs will need to be made to extend service life. Deferred elements may require more vigilance from a monitoring and maintenance standpoint. The conceptual repair recommendations in Appendix C are based on extrapolation of information obtained during this assessment. Depending on Association decisions, further assessment work may be warranted to better determine extent of problems and develop a more precise scope of work.

The conceptual scope of repair addresses key details that exhibited the main performance issues, addressed community-wide for planning and pricing purposes. The scope is detailed enough to send to qualified contractors for order-of-magnitude pricing; however, **it is not meant for construction**. Cost information from contractors will allow the Association to identify priorities and develop a strategy, budget and refined scope for future repairs.

### Building Envelope Principles (general background)

**General.** The main function of the building envelope is to serve as an environmental separator between outdoor and indoor environmental conditions. The envelope should resist environmental loads (wind, solar gain, etc.) and also account for interior temperature and humidity (primarily controlled by occupants). In the Pacific Northwest, the envelope should accommodate proper water management using durable materials, resist mechanisms of deterioration, and have compatible adjoining materials.

**Mechanism of Deterioration.** The mechanism of failure that needs to be primarily addressed is management of exterior moisture. Components must be designed for an expected design service life. Address external moisture sources:

- **Precipitation.** In the Pacific Northwest, rainfall is the primary environmental factor. The envelope must minimize ingress through four strategies:
  - **Deflection** — first line of protection (overhangs, protective flashings, wall cladding).
  - **Drainage** — wall cladding cavity for capillary break and to direct moisture out; air-circulation improves drying.
  - **Drying** — natural drying capacity.
  - **Durability** — concrete, metal, pressure treated wood for water-penetration resistance.

- **Air Leakage.** Air pressure differentials can draw moist warm interior air outward (or rainwater inward) leading to condensation or water deposition. Controlled by an air-barrier system. Air-tightness of walls, windows and exterior penetrations is essential.

- **Vapor Diffusion.** Differences in vapor pressure (winter especially) drive diffusion. A vapor retarder/barrier (polyethylene, vapor-resistant PVA primer, faced insulations, self-adhered membrane, metal backpans, glass) controls diffusion. In Pacific Northwest, the vapor retarder should be installed inward from thermal insulation (warm side).

- **Heat Transfer.** Resisted by insulation. Proper placement of vapor retarder relative to insulation also minimizes condensation.

- **Other.** Surface water and ground moisture should not be allowed to accumulate at building base.

- **Interior Environment.** RH should be kept within limits to reduce condensation. For human comfort, RH between roughly **30%–60%**. To reduce risk of interior moisture causing condensation at windows or in wall/roof assemblies, RH should not exceed 60%.

In the Pacific Northwest climate, regular use of bath/kitchen exhaust fans is the best method for keeping interior RH low. Fans should run during showers and for at least one hour after; clothes dryers must work effectively (clean lint traps; clean ducts/vents). Condensation on windows is an easy indicator of unacceptable interior conditions. Suggested activities to reduce interior RH:
- Regular bath fan use during/after showering.
- Regular kitchen fan use during/after cooking.
- Keep curtains/drapes/blinds open to allow circulation over windows.
- Open windows for natural ventilation.
- Avoid placing furniture tightly against exterior walls (blocks air).
- Avoid excessive amounts of potted plants.

## 10. BEAR Consulting Services — Supplemental Building Envelope Investigation

**Date:** May 10, 2017 (v1.2)
**Prepared for:** Summit Ridge HOA, 1–21 Summit Ridge Court, Lake Oswego, OR
**Prepared by:** BEAR Consulting Services LLC, 13500 SW Pacific Hwy Ste 58, 58-220, Tigard, OR 97223 (OR CCB# 209436), www.bearcs.com

### Executive Summary
On **May 4, 2017**, BEAR Consulting Services LLC ("BEAR") performed a Building Envelope Investigation of Summit Ridge Townhomes ("the Project") in general conformance with **ASTM E2128** "Standard Guide for Evaluating Water Leakage of Building Walls." Intent: evaluate existing condition of components comprising Project's building envelope at limited locations, determine areas of elevated risk of water intrusion, document water leakage, water leakage pathways, and resultant property damage. Information will assist Project ownership in determining options for an adequate scope of repair and maintenance plan.

### Scope
Scope was limited to observing/evaluating selected as-built construction assemblies or building locations, as they existed at the time of the on-site evaluation. Scope: 21 town homes in 6 buildings comprise the Project. Evaluation provides selected examples of concerning conditions; assemblies/locations not specifically identified were beyond the scope. Limited to:
- Visual review of exterior wall coverings and window assemblies.
- Visual review of roof assemblies as seen from the ground or a ladder.
- Examination of concealed building envelope assemblies at **ten (10) specific exploratory inspection opening locations**.

### Observations: Systemic Concerns
BEAR noted multiple and systemic conditions failing to provide adequate weather protection and facilitating water infiltration behind exterior coverings or absorption by moisture-sensitive components. Conditions are not in accordance with industry standard for installation, manufacturer's installation specifications, and/or the Oregon Building Code.

BEAR also observed systemic conditions throughout the Project resulting in negated or impeded incidental water egress to the exterior once water infiltration into underlying assemblies has occurred — facilitating moisture accumulation and saturation within the building envelope.

When repairs are eventually put in place, the components throughout the Project will need to be installed in conformance with current and applicable product manufacturer's installation requirements, industry association standards, and building codes.

Examples of systemic concerns (not exhaustive):
- Improper installation of vinyl window assemblies.
- Omitted and/or improper installation of dynamic sealant joint.
- Improper siding installation.
- Inadequate or improperly dimensioned flashing.
- Un-primed field cut edges of trim members.
- Omitted up-turned end dam flashing termination.
- Improper flashing integration with WRB.
- Negated or improper clearance between siding termination and horizontal surface of metal flashing.
- Improper and/or omitted flexible flashing installation.
- Improper WRB installation.
- Omitted and/or discontinuous WRB.
- Inadequate air leakage control provisions.
- Inadequate and/or omitted flexible flashing membrane at rough openings.
- Omitted and/or improper integration of roof underlayment with adjacent vertical wall assemblies.

### Evidence of Water Intrusion and Resultant Damage
Conditions noted in the preceding section, individually or in combination, resulted in observed conditions and damage throughout all buildings due to sustained presence of moisture within the building envelope and/or improper installation of materials:
- Moisture-stained wall assembly components.
- Microbial/fungal growth on wall assemblies.
- Microbial growth on WRB and gypsum sheathing.
- Corroded fasteners or accessories within wall assembly.
- Elevated moisture content in trim and gypsum sheathing.
- Deteriorated gypsum sheathing.
- Elevated moisture and/or dry rot wood framing.
- Elevated moisture and/or dry rot in trim members.

### Recommended Next Steps
BEAR believes the systemic nonconforming and water leakage-facilitating conditions identified need to be remediated to ensure the longevity and weather-resistance of the Project. Recommend pursuing the following in a timely manner:
- **Scope of Repair:** Have BEAR generate a scope of repair, providing an outline of steps necessary to remediate identified conditions. Repair scope can then be used to solicit bids from qualified repair contractors.
- **Contractor Bid Analysis and Selection:** Have BEAR assist the Board in determining qualified contractors to participate in the bidding process, assist with pre-bid site walks, bid analysis, and contractor selection.
