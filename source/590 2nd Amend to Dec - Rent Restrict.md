## SECOND AMENDMENT TO DECLARATION — SUMMITRIDGE TOWNHOMES HOMEOWNERS ASSOCIATION

This Second Amendment to the Declaration is made on the date indicated below by Summitridge Townhomes Homeowners Association, an Oregon nonprofit corporation. This amendment is effective upon recording in the Records of Multnomah County, Oregon.

WHEREAS certain real property in Multnomah County, Oregon was subjected to a Declaration of Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions ("Declaration") recorded on December 17, 1999 as Recording No. 99-225640 and amended by First Amendment to Declaration recorded on September 2, 2016 as Recording No. 2016-110181;

WHEREAS not less than a majority of the Board of Directors of the Summitridge Townhomes Homeowners Association approved the amendment described herein, and, after notice to all of the Association members entitled to vote thereon, not less than 75% of the voting power of the Association consented in writing to the amendment described herein; and

WHEREAS, the Declaration may be amended pursuant to Article XI of the Declaration;

NOW THEREFORE, the undersigned do certify that Article V of the Declaration is amended to include a new section 1.M as follows:

### M. Rental Restrictions

(1) **"Rent"** shall mean to grant a lease, right to use, or right to occupy a Living Unit to a person(s) other than the Owner for a specified or unspecified term in exchange for payment whether it be in the form of money, property, or other goods or services of value. Any written agreement describing such rental is referenced herein as either a "rental agreement" or a "lease." "Rent" shall not mean or include joint ownership of a Living Unit by means of joint tenancy, tenancy-in-common, or other forms of co-ownership. It also shall not mean or include roommate-type agreements where the Owner continues to occupy the Living Unit.

(2) **Rental of Living Units.**

(a) Not more than 5 of the Living Units may be rented at any given time. Before a Living Unit may be rented by an Owner, the Owner notify the Board of intent to lease and must obtain written approval from the Board of Directors. Within 5 business days of such notification by Owner, the Board shall advise the Owner whether such proposed tenancy would exceed the maximum number of rented Living Units under this section and whether approval is granted.

(b) If the maximum of 5 Living Units are already rented, an Owner may rent his or her Living Unit only if the Board of Directors determines that a hardship exists which justifies permitting the additional rental as a hardship exemption.

(i) In determining whether a hardship exists, the Board of Directors may consider medical, employment, educational, or financial issues affecting the Owner.

(ii) Hardship rentals may be allowed for a period not to exceed 2 years per Living Unit per hardship.

(iii) Determination of the existence of a hardship is within the discretion of the Board of Directors, and the Board of Directors is authorized to adopt additional procedures relating to consideration of hardship requests.

(iv) If the Living Unit is owned by a mortgagee following foreclosure or deed in lieu of foreclosure, the Living Unit may be rented during the period of such ownership.

(v) If a proposed rental is prohibited because the maximum number of Living Units are already rented, the Board shall place the Owner on a waiting list and shall notify such Owner when such owner's Living Unit may be rented. The waiting list shall be processed on a first-come, first-served basis.

(c) Once an Owner is notified that his or her Living Unit may be rented, such Owner, within 60 days from the date of such notice, may enter into a lease with a tenant subject to the requirements set forth herein. If a notified Owner has not entered into a lease within such period, the Owner must reapply to the Board with a new request to rent his or her Living Unit.

(d) An Owner who receives permission from the Board to rent his or her Living Unit may continue to rent such Living Unit upon the expiration or termination of such tenancy, provided that if the Living Unit becomes owner-occupied or vacant for any period exceeding 60 days, the Owner may no longer rent the Living Unit and must reapply to the Board with a new request to rent his or her Living Unit.

(3) **Lease or Rental Agreement Requirements.**

(a) All leases and rental agreements must be in writing, must prohibit sublets, and shall provide that the terms thereof shall be subject in all respects to the provisions of the Declaration, Bylaws, and all rules and regulations adopted by the Board; and that any failure by the-tenant to comply with the terms of such documents or rules and regulations shall be considered a default under the lease or rental agreement;

(b) The Living Unit may not be rented for transient or hotel purposes, and all leases and rental agreements shall be for an initial term of not less than 6 months, with no less than 30 day subsequent terms thereafter;

(c) The rental must be for the entire Living Unit and not merely parts of the unit;

(d) Any Owner who rents a Living Unit shall obtain and maintain in full force and effect an insurance policy providing coverage for liability related to, arising out of or resulting from the tenant's occupancy or use of the unit and common area, with limits as set from time to time by the Board of Directors. Such policy shall name the Association as an additional insured party.

(e) If an Owner fails to follow the procedures set forth in this section with respect to the rental of his or her Living Unit, then at any time after learning of such rental, the Board of Directors may charge such Owner an administrative fee, the amount of which shall be determined from time to time by the Board of Directors. The purpose of the fee is to reimburse the Association for time, costs and expenses, including–management time incurred obtaining information about the tenant and providing such tenant with copies of Association documents. Charging an Owner an administrative fee or providing such Owner's tenant with copies of Association documents, however, shall not bar or limit the Association's remedies arising from such Owner's or Tenant's violations of the provisions of the Declaration, Bylaws, and Rules and Regulations. Such fee, if unpaid, shall become an assessment against the Living Unit.

(f) All Owners who rent their Living Units shall promptly notify the Association in writing of all tenants and occupants of such units and shall provide the Association with a complete copy of the lease or rental agreement. All Owners renting their Living Units shall promptly notify the Association of the address and telephone number where such owner can be reached.

(4) **Enforcement.** In addition to any other remedy at law or equity, the Association may enforce violations of this Section by imposition of a fine pursuant to a schedule of fines adopted by the Board, and subject to notice and hearing as provided in the Act, the Declaration, Bylaws, and Rules and Regulations of the Association. Such fines, if unpaid, shall become an assessment against the Living Unit of the Owner.

(5) **Pre-existing Leases; exception from policy.** For a Living Unit under an existing rental agreement as of the effective date of this Amendment, the Owner may continue such lease or renewals thereof without compliance with these lease restrictions under the following conditions:

(a) A unit owner who, as of the effective date, is renting or leasing a unit for a term of at least thirty (30) days has a "Grandfather Lease" and may continue to rent or lease the unit to the existing tenant.

(b) If a Grandfather Lease is terminated, the unit owner shall apply to the board of directors for approval to rent or lease the unit thereafter in accordance with Section 1.M(3) of this article unless the unit owner rents or leases the unit within sixty (60) days of termination of the Grandfather Lease.

(c) The right of a unit owner to rent or lease a unit under this section terminates when the unit owner no longer owns the unit. The successor in interest to the unit has no rights under this section (whether acquired by sale, foreclosure, rights of inheritance, assignment, or any other means).

(d) ORS Chapter 90 Not Applicable. Nothing in this article may be construed to impose on the Association the duties, responsibilities, or liabilities of a landlord under ORS chapter 90 or subject the Association to any requirements of ORS chapter 90.

The terms of this Amendment shall control over and implicitly amend any inconsistent provision of the Declaration or Bylaws. Except as specifically amended by this instrument, all provisions of the Declaration shall remain in full force and effect.
