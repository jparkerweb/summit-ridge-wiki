# Architectural Review

## Authority

Architectural and design control is governed by **CC&Rs Article VII** and **Bylaws Article X**.

The Architectural Committee (referred to interchangeably as the "AC", "ARC", or "Architectural Review Committee") may act for the Board and Declarant to the extent set forth in the Declaration (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 1).

## Composition

- Initially: as many persons as Declarant appoints (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 1; Bylaws Article X § 1).
- After Declarant transfers selection authority to the Board: the Committee consists of **at least three persons** (Source: Bylaws Article X § 1).
- Authority transferred to the Board on the earlier of (i) Declarant's written election to transfer, or (ii) closing of the sale of all Lots to third parties by Declarant (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 1).
- After transfer or after the Turnover Meeting, the Board appoints and removes Committee members; **if the Board fails to appoint, the Board itself serves as the Committee** (Source: Bylaws Article X § 1).

## When AC Approval Is Required

> **No improvements shall be commenced, erected, placed, altered, or maintained on any Lot by an owner, until the design plans and specifications showing the nature, shape, heights, materials, colors, and proposed location of the improvement have been submitted and approved by the Architectural Committee.** (Source: Rules and Regulations § Architecture)

The Declaration adds that this applies to any **exterior addition to or change or alteration** of a Lot (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 1).

## Submission Procedure

For any homeowner wishing to make an addition, alteration, or improvement to the exterior of their dwelling or to Common Elements:

1. Complete an **Architectural Request Form**.
2. Submit the form to the Architectural Committee.
3. The Architectural Committee reviews the application.
4. The Committee has up to **90 days** from the date the complete written application is submitted to respond.

(Source: Rules and Regulations § Architecture)

The Declaration further states: **if the AC fails to approve or disapprove within 90 days after submission, approval will not be required and Article VII will be deemed fully complied with** (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 1).

## Form Fields (Architecture Request)

- Date
- Homeowner
- Address
- Day Phone / Evening Phone
- Brief description of improvement (attach drawing, photo, or specs)
- Name of Company Making Improvements / Street / City / State / Zip / Phone
- (Below "Do Not Write" line) Date of Review by Architectural Committee, Recommendation, Committee Decision (Approved/Denied)

A **Landscape Committee — Request for Improvements** form has the same field structure but is routed to and signed by the Landscape Committee (Source: Rules and Regulations § Forms).

## Owner-Responsibility Repairs

Per the [Maintenance Responsibility Resolution (Nov 1, 2016)](maintenance-responsibility.md): before an Owner undertakes repair or replacement of any component that is the Owner's responsibility and located on the **exterior** of the building, that Owner must submit an application to the ARC. The ARC may request, among other items, **evidence from a construction professional that the proposed repair will not adversely impact the weatherproofing of the building envelope**. Imposing this or other requirements is solely within the discretion of the ARC (Source: Rules and Regulations § Board Resolution Nov 2016 — Resolved).

## Right of Entry / Inspection

Any agent or officer of the Association may at any reasonable hour during construction or remodeling **enter and inspect any part of the Property to determine compliance**, and is not deemed guilty of trespass. The Association may issue a Certificate of Completion and Compliance (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 3).

## Records and Conclusive Evidence

Records of the Association regarding compliance are conclusive evidence as to all matters shown by such records. Issuance of a Certificate of Completion and Compliance by the Secretary or Assistant Secretary is conclusive evidence — protecting any title insurer and fully protecting any purchaser or encumbrancer (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 4).

After expiration of one year following the issuance of a building permit, **any structure, work, improvement, or alteration is deemed in compliance unless** a notice of non-compliance has been recorded by the Association in the Multnomah County Clerk's office, or unless legal proceedings have been instituted to enforce compliance or completion (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 4).
