# Collections and Delinquency

The Board adopted a uniform collection procedure on **October 20, 2017** ("Collection Resolution"), engaging **Vial Fotheringham LLP ("VF")** of Lake Oswego as collection counsel under a "CFE Agreement" (Source: Rules and Regulations § Board Resolution Oct 2017, Recitals).

For the underlying late-fee, interest, and lien provisions (CC&Rs Article IV § 8 as amended), see [Assessments and Fees § Late Charges and Interest](assessments-and-fees.md#late-charges-and-interest).

## Trigger for Delinquency

An assessment is **delinquent if not paid within 30 days** of the due date (annual assessments are due January 1) (Source: Collection Resolution § 1.5(c); CC&Rs Article IV §§ 6, 8.B).

## Charges Imposed

| Charge | Amount | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Interest | **10% per annum** from due date | CC&Rs Article IV § 8.A; Collection Resolution § 2.1 |
| Late charge | **20% of the assessment** (Board may set up to 30%) | CC&Rs Article IV § 8.D as amended; Collection Resolution § 2.2 |
| Management company collection charges | At cost per management contract — mailing, recording, account maintenance | Collection Resolution § 2.3(a) |
| Attorney fees | All work performed (intake, calculations, calls, research, drafting, depositions, trial prep, travel, investigations, court appearances, post-judgment) | Collection Resolution § 2.3(b) |
| Other costs | Recording, postage, copy, service, court, filing, paralegal, private investigator, garnishment fees | Collection Resolution § 2.3(c) |

All collection costs are **due when incurred** and are enforceable as assessments (Source: Collection Resolution § 2.3; CC&Rs Article IV § 8.D as amended).

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Association Notice of Claim of Lien (optional)
When an assessment is levied, the Association or its agent **may** record a notice of claim of lien in the Records of Multnomah County, Oregon (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.1).

### Step 2 — Association Payment Demand Letter
When an assessment is delinquent, the Association sends the Owner a written payment demand letter substantially in the form of Exhibit A, including:
- (a) Statement of the amount due
- (b) Demand for immediate payment
- (c) Notice that, if unpaid by the turnover date stated, the account will be turned over to VF
- (d) Statement that the Owner is responsible for all collection costs, which constitute assessments

(Source: Collection Resolution § 3.2)

### Step 3 — Turnover to VF
If the assessment remains unpaid after the turnover date in the Association Payment Demand Letter, the account is turned over to VF (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.3(a)).

> **After turnover, all contact and communications regarding the assessment account must be with VF**, unless VF gives written consent otherwise (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.3(b)).

### Step 4 — Initial Notice from VF + Recording
On receiving the file, VF:
- (a) Sends a written **Initial Notice** of delinquent balance to the Owner
- (b) Prepares and records the Notice of Claim of Lien if not already recorded

(Source: Collection Resolution § 3.4)

### Step 5 — Final VF Payment Demand
If unpaid by the date in the Initial Notice (and no payment plan arranged), VF sends a final demand letter requiring payment of all moneys due **within 10 days** (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.5).

### Step 6 — Legal Action
If still unpaid, VF initiates either:
- A lawsuit for a personal money judgment, or
- Foreclosure of the Association lien against the Lot.

(Source: Collection Resolution § 3.6; CC&Rs Article IV §§ 8.A, 8.B(2)–(3))

### Step 7 — Execution / Enforcement of Judgment
After judgment, VF may collect by any combination of:
- (a) Garnishing the Owner's bank account
- (b) Garnishing the Owner's wages
- (c) Executing a writ against real or personal property
- (d) Any other legally authorized method

(Source: Collection Resolution § 3.7)

## Payment Plans

After turnover, payment plans must be approved by **both VF and the Board** (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.8).

## Disbursal of Funds (50/50 Split)

After turnover, payments received are split **50/50** between two expense categories until one is paid in full:
- 50% applied to past-due assessments and non-attorney collection charges/fees
- 50% applied to attorney-related charges/fees

After one category is paid in full, all remaining payments go to the other category (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.10).

## Owner Responsibilities

- Owners must ensure assessments are paid when due, regardless of any procedure prescribed (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.11(a)).
- Owners must **notify the Association in writing of any change of mailing address** (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.11(b)).

## Other Remedies

The Collection Resolution does not limit the Association's other remedies — including termination of utilities and preventing the Owner from access to recreational or service facilities — if provided under the Declaration, Bylaws, Rules and Regulations, or the Oregon Planned Community Act (Source: Collection Resolution § 3.11(c)). The Association may **not** deprive any Owner of access to and from the Owner's Lot (Source: CC&Rs Article IV § 8.B(1)).

## Notice to First Mortgagees

For any delinquency continuing **60 days**, the Board must notify any Eligible Holder of a first mortgage on that Lot (Source: CC&Rs Article XIII § 1.C; Article IV § 8.C).
