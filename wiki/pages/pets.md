# Pets

## Allowed Pets

The Declaration permits **only domestic dogs or cats and caged birds kept within the dwelling house**. No animals or fowl may be raised, kept, or permitted on the Property otherwise. Pets may not be kept, bred, or raised for commercial purposes (Source: CC&Rs Article V § 1.B).

## Number Limit

- **CC&Rs Article V § 1.B (strict text)**: "no more than **one (1)** of any such pets shall be permitted to be kept on any Lot."
- **Rules and Regulations § Pets (community practice)**: "No animal shall be kept, bred, or raised for commercial purposes or in **unreasonable numbers**" — no numeric limit specified.

**In practice the one-pet cap is treated as a soft limit.** Multiple-pet households (including more than one dog) are present in the community and have not been actively enforced against. The CC&R text remains on the books and could in principle be invoked by the Board, but day-to-day expectation is the "no unreasonable numbers" standard from the Handbook.

If pet count ever becomes an issue (e.g., a complaint to the Board), the Declaration text would control in a formal enforcement action per Bylaws Article XXI. Owners considering more than one pet should still avoid creating noise, damage, or nuisance issues that could trigger a complaint.

## Conduct Rules

- All pets must be **carried or kept on a leash** while outside (Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets).
- Dogs, cats, and pet birds may not be permitted to run at large (Source: CC&Rs Article V § 1.B).
- All pets must be **registered and inoculated as required by law** (Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets).
- Pet owners must **properly clean up after their pets** (Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets).
- No pet may be permitted to cause or create a nuisance, unreasonable disturbance, or noise (Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets).

## Damage

All damage created by a pet is the **sole responsibility of the pet owner**. Damage to property will be repaired by the Association and **cost billed to the property owner** (Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets).

## Animals at Large (City)

According to City of Lake Oswego ordinance, animals at large may be taken into custody by the city and disposed of in accordance with the city ordinance for disposition of personal property or the impoundment of dogs (Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets — Complaint Procedure).

## Complaint Procedure

If a pet is being offensive:
1. Offended party should first talk with the pet owner and request correction.
2. If the offense continues, submit written details to the Managing Agent or Board of Directors — explaining the offense, time, place, pet description, owner's name, and address.

(Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets — Complaint Procedure)

## Notices and Fines

| Offense | Action |
|---|---|
| **First** | Violation notice from Managing Agent and/or Board of Directors |
| **Second / Consecutive** | Violation notice plus fine of **$15.00 per occurrence** |

(Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets — Notices and Fines)

For general enforcement framework, see [Enforcement and Fines](enforcement-and-fines.md).
