# Common Areas

## What Counts as Common Area

Per the Rules and Regulations, the following are considered **Common Areas**:
- Private streets
- Parking areas
- Retaining walls
- Curbs
- Gutters
- Utilities thereon
- Landscaped areas

(Source: Rules and Regulations § Common Elements — Common Areas)

The Declaration uses **"Common Properties"** to mean areas of land intended for the common use, enjoyment, and/or benefit of all Members, including the **[Private Open Space Tracts](private-open-space-and-wetlands.md)** designated as Tracts "A" and "B" (Source: CC&Rs Article I.F).

## Easement of Enjoyment

Subject to the restrictions in Article III § 3, every Member has an **easement and right of enjoyment** in and to the Common Properties; such easement is appurtenant to and passes with the title to every Lot upon recordation of a contract of sale (Source: CC&Rs Article III § 1).

## Restrictions on Members' Easements

The rights of enjoyment are subject to:

- **A. Limit on guests**: The Association may limit the number of guests of Members.
- **B. Suspension**: The Association may suspend a Member's enjoyment rights for any period during which any assessment remains unpaid, and for any period **not to exceed 30 days for any infraction** of published rules and regulations.
- **C. Mortgage**: The Declarant and Association may mortgage the Common Properties as security for an improvement loan, subject to subordination to the Declaration and Plat.
- **D. Dedication / Transfer**: The Association may dedicate or transfer Common Properties to a public agency, subject to Member vote and **written approval by the City of Lake Oswego**.
- **E. Declarant utility easements**: Declarant may grant easements to public agencies/utilities as required to develop the Property, subject to City review and protection of trees in the Private Open Space Tracts.
- **F. Rules and regulations**: Declarant or Association may adopt reasonable rules for use, subject to City approval.
- **G. Tracts A and B**: The Private Open Space Tracts may be used **only for passive recreational purposes** such as walking and picnicking; no other use is permitted.

(Source: CC&Rs Article III § 3)

## Title to Common Properties

The Declarant could retain legal title to the Common Properties until improvements thereon were completed and the Association was able to maintain them in Declarant's judgment. The Declarant **was required to convey** the Common Properties to the Association no later than **December 31, 2001** (Source: CC&Rs Article III § 2).

## Maintenance

The Association is responsible for **maintaining and repairing** the Common Properties — including the Private Open Space Tracts — funded by assessments. **The City of Lake Oswego has the right to enforce these maintenance and repair obligations** at the expense of the Association and Owners (Source: CC&Rs Article III § 4; Article XII § 1).

The Association maintains exclusive management and control of the Common Properties and all improvements therein, subject to the requirement that **Tract A shall be maintained in its natural condition** (Source: CC&Rs Article XII § 1).

For specific allocation of maintenance items, see [Maintenance Responsibility](maintenance-responsibility.md).

## Right of Entry / Common-Use Property

The Association may acquire, hold, and dispose of tangible and intangible personal property and real property. The Board, on behalf of the Association, accepts any Common Properties conveyed by the Declarant (Source: CC&Rs Article XII § 3).

The Association may enter Lots for **emergency, security, or safety purposes**. Except in emergencies, entry is only during reasonable hours and after reasonable notice (Source: CC&Rs Article XII § 4).

## Easements Reserved (Article VIII)

- Declarant grants the City and reserves to itself perpetual easements under, over, and across portions of the Property designated on the Plat as **utility easements** for sewers, drainage, and utility services (Source: CC&Rs Article VIII § 1).
- Declarant grants the Association easements over, under, and upon the Common Area for: utility installation/maintenance, construction/maintenance/repair/use of the Building Structure and Common Area, and repairs to existing structures or Building Structures (Source: CC&Rs Article VIII § 2).
- Declarant grants the City and other public agencies the right to construct, replace, enlarge, and maintain public facilities along public easements. **The City/agency is not responsible for replacing landscaping, fencing, structures, shrubs, or trees** placed within public easements. The City must give adequate notice (Source: CC&Rs Article VIII § 3).

## No Curbside / Street Parking

To allow emergency vehicles and owner access to driveways, **there is no curbside / street parking within the community**. See [Parking](parking.md) (Source: Rules and Regulations § Parking — Curb Side / Street Parking; CC&Rs Article V § 1.J).

## Snow and Ice on Common Area

The Association has authority (but is not required) to arrange for snow and ice removal from the Common Area, **except Tract A shall not be disturbed**. The Association does not remove snow/ice from individual Lots (Source: CC&Rs Article II § 3.E(1)).

## Enforcement of Common-Element Infractions

If an infraction occurs, the Board requests the owner to remove the infraction and repair or return the common area to original state, **at the cost of the owner**. If the owner does not respond, the management company is contacted and further action is taken (Source: Rules and Regulations § Common Elements — Enforcement Procedures).
