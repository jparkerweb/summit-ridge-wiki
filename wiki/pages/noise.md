# Noise

## General Standard

Owners or occupants must **exercise extreme care not to make noises which may disturb other owners**, including the use of musical instruments, radios, televisions, and amplifiers (Source: Rules and Regulations § Noise).

The Declaration also prohibits any noxious or offensive activity, or anything which may become an annoyance or nuisance to the neighborhood or detract from its value as a residential district (Source: CC&Rs Article V § 1.D).

## Quiet Time

**Quiet Time** is observed between **10:00 PM and 8:00 AM**. During this time:
- No device designed for sound production (radios, televisions, musical instruments, stereos) may be used so that the sound is heard by neighbors or in the common area.
- No gatherings (outdoors or in common areas) that create excess noises from collective voices.

(Source: Rules and Regulations § Noise — Quiet Time)

## City of Lake Oswego Code (referenced)

Per City of Lake Oswego Codes Section 34.10.537 — Unnecessary Noise — examples of violations:

- Use of a motor vehicle to create loud and unnecessary noise.
- Use or operation of any device designed for sound production or reproduction (radios, TVs, musical instruments, loud speakers) **between 10:00 PM and 7:00 AM**.
- Gathering of any number of persons (public or private premises) creating excess noise from collective voices **between 10:00 PM and 7:00 AM**. "Excessive noise" means noise plainly audible within any dwelling unit not the source of the sound.

For complete listing, refer to City of Lake Oswego Codes, Section 34.10.537 (Source: Rules and Regulations § Noise).

> **Note**: The HOA's Quiet Time (10 PM – 8 AM) is one hour longer in the morning than the City code (10 PM – 7 AM).

## Pets

Pet owners must not allow pets to cause noise that creates a nuisance or unreasonable disturbance (Source: Rules and Regulations § Pets; CC&Rs Article V § 1.D). See [Pets](pets.md).

## Enforcement

The offended party should:
1. First **talk with the home owner/tenant** and request that the noise be stopped.
2. If noise persists, **contact the City of Lake Oswego Code Services and file a noise complaint**.
3. If it is an emergency, **call 911**.

(Source: Rules and Regulations § Noise — Enforcement Procedures)
