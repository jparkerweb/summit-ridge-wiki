# Landscaping

Owners may enhance flowerbeds with plantings at their own expense, but must coordinate with the existing landscape and follow [Architectural Review](architectural-review.md) for additions or alterations. Major changes — particularly tree removal — also implicate the City of Lake Oswego Tree Code.

## Personal Plantings

- Homeowners are encouraged to enhance flowerbeds with seasonal plantings **at their own expense** (Source: Rules and Regulations § Landscaping — Plants).
- Homeowners must, to the best of their ability, **maintain the uniformity of the existing landscaping** when planting their own plants (Source: Rules and Regulations § Landscaping — Plants).
- Upkeep of personal plantings falls on the homeowner. **All dead plantings should be removed promptly** (Source: Rules and Regulations § Landscaping — Plants).
- The landscapers will not be held liable for altering plantings planted by the homeowner (e.g., through pruning, watering, etc.) (Source: Rules and Regulations § Landscaping — Plants).

## Flower Pots, Window Boxes, Deck Pots

- Homeowners may place flowers along deck, patios, and entryway (Source: Rules and Regulations § Landscaping — Flower pots).
- Homeowner is responsible for any **damage caused to common area or to the exterior of the building** while installing flowers (Source: Rules and Regulations § Landscaping — Flower pots).
- Planters on deck or deck rails require **footed pots, saucers, or space under pots for ventilation** to prevent accelerated rotting or damage to wood or staining of cement (Source: Rules and Regulations § Landscaping — Flower pots).

## Landscaping on the Lot

- Each Owner must provide and maintain landscaping on the Owner's Lot of such a nature and quality as to **not detract from the aesthetic value** of the SummitRidge Townhomes Property (Source: CC&Rs Article VII § 2.J).
- It is the intent of the Declaration that Lots, including vacant Lots, be maintained in a reasonably presentable condition (Source: CC&Rs Article IX § 1).

## Landscape Plan Approval

- Any landscaping to be undertaken by a Homeowner on the individual lot is **subject to the approval of the Summit Ridge Homeowners Association** (Source: CC&Rs Article IX § 2).
- A **Landscape Committee — Request for Improvements** form is provided (Source: Rules and Regulations § Forms).
- Where the HOA has permitted a Homeowner to plant a portion of the Common Properties (excluding Tracts A and B), in accordance with a landscaping plan submitted prior to the work, **the homeowner is thenceforth obligated to maintain at their own expense** such planting (Source: CC&Rs Article IX § 2).
- Failure of the Owner to maintain gives the Association the right, upon reasonable notice, to **maintain such area and charge the expense to the Owner** (Source: CC&Rs Article IX § 2).

## Trees

### City of Lake Oswego Tree Code
The Lots and Common Property are subject to the **Tree Code of the City of Lake Oswego**. **No trees may be removed** without complying with the Tree Code and CC&Rs Article V (Source: CC&Rs Article V § 1.K).

### Removal Standards
- All trees (other than within the public right-of-way) must be preserved and may only be removed after they have been shown to be **hazardous by a certified arborist**, and after a tree-cutting permit has been obtained from the City (Source: CC&Rs Article V § 1.H).
- A detailed landscape plan must be submitted along with any tree-cutting application for City review and approval (Source: CC&Rs Article V § 1.H).
- The City may recommend revisions to preserve the treed character of the neighborhood (Source: CC&Rs Article V § 1.H).
- Trees within the public right-of-way may be removed at the discretion of the governmental body (Source: CC&Rs Article V § 1.H).

### Street Trees
Street trees are a requirement of the City of Lake Oswego. An Owner must have **approval of the Summit Ridge Homeowners Association and the City of Lake Oswego** prior to any movement or replacement of street trees (Source: CC&Rs Article IX § 2).

### Tract A (Wetlands Buffer)
Tract A is intended to be maintained in its **natural condition** and there shall be **no changes** in this Tract by an individual Homeowner. See [Private Open Space and Wetlands](private-open-space-and-wetlands.md) (Source: CC&Rs Article IX § 2; Article V § 1.F).

## Association Right of Entry for Landscape Maintenance

The Association has the right at all times to enter upon any Lot after reasonable notice to the Owner, to:
- Remove debris, weeds, or other waste material
- Trim or, if cut back, remove if damaged or dead
- Cultivate and/or maintain planting beds, ground cover, hedges, trees, shrubs, plants, or lawns
- **Charge the expense to the Owner as an assessment**

(Source: CC&Rs Article IX § 1)
